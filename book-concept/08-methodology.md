# Methodology Appendix

## How This Concept Package Was Built — and How to Audit It

This appendix documents the process that produced the book-concept package for *Your Kid's AI Best Friend* (working title). It is written for a stranger — an editor, investor, or prospective author — who needs to know not just what the package claims, but how those claims were generated, stress-tested, and corrected, and where the remaining soft spots are. The package was produced in July 2026 by a multi-agent AI research pipeline (see Section 5, AI-Generation Disclosure). Nothing in this appendix should be read as a substitute for the independent verification pass described in Section 3.

---

## 1. The Four-Phase Pipeline

### Phase 1 — Parallel Market Sweep (7 scouts → 30 raw opportunities → ranked top 8)

Seven independent research scouts ran simultaneous web-research sweeps, each assigned a different discovery angle: forum pain (reader venting and community distress signals), industry trends (Circana/Publishers Weekly category data and agent wish-lists), retail gaps (shelf autopsies of stale incumbents), search demand (news-coverage-to-book-gap analysis), demographic shifts, social reading (BookTok/genre-fiction signals), and expert niches. Each scout ran roughly 10–18 distinct web searches and reported only opportunities it could tie to real, cited URLs, with per-item confidence ratings and honest dead-end logs.

The seven scouts produced 30 raw opportunities. A synthesis pass then deduplicated and merged them, explicitly crediting cross-validation when multiple scouts converged on the same signal from non-overlapping evidence, and ranked a top eight with full source lists plus a documented discard rationale for everything cut. The eventual winning topic — a parent's protocol guide to teen AI-companion attachment — was the strongest convergence in the sweep: three of seven scouts (forum-pain, retail-gaps, search-demand) surfaced it independently through different evidence types. The eldercare-triage opportunity drew three-scout convergence as well; the GLP-1 aftermath and graduate-employment opportunities each drew two.

### Phase 2 — Concept Tournament (6 concepts, 3-persona judge panel, 5-criteria scoring)

Six of the top eight opportunities were developed into full book concepts — working title, one-line pitch, chapter-level table of contents, comp titles, author profile, marketing hooks, and self-declared open questions. (The two ranked opportunities that did not advance to concept development were the post-AI graduate-employment playbook, ranked 7, and the post-dating-app offline-romance guide, ranked 8.)

Each concept was then scored by a three-persona judge panel — an acquiring editor, a literary agent, and a marketer — across five criteria: reader pain, urgency/why-now, originality, commercial ceiling, and buildability. Each judge scored each criterion out of 10, for a maximum of 150 points per concept. Judges also filed narrative rationales and a named kill risk per concept. *The Perfect Listener* (the teen-AI-companion concept, later retitled) won at 124/150, with all three judges scoring it within one point of each other; two of the three judges named it their outright acquisition. The winner was then refined, deliberately grafting structural assets from the losing concepts (crisis-triage front section, named-protocol flag-planting, QR-updated appendix architecture, multi-copy household mechanics). The full score table and losing-concept record appear in Section 2.

### Phase 3 — Adversarial Adjudication (5 skeptics + 6 fact-checkers)

The refined winner was then handed to an adversarial review layer whose explicit job was to kill it: five skeptics, each prosecuting a different failure theory (preemption, demand, durability, evidence, execution), plus six fact-checkers who ran every load-bearing claim to ground with primary-source citations. The output — which **overrides the tournament-stage concept wherever the two conflict** — was:

- a proceed-with-revisions verdict ("the niche is real and the framing around it is false");
- **11 mandatory concept revisions**, each with the refuting evidence named;
- **17 corrected facts**, each with a canonical citable wording and sources — the only stat wordings the package is permitted to use;
- a **10-item risk register** with severity, likelihood, and mitigation;
- a record of **seven kill scenarios prosecuted and survived** (announced-rival, elite-panic, fad-decay, stale-news-cycle, settings-toggle, legal-evisceration, and junk-evidence attacks).

The adjudication materially changed the concept. The most consequential corrections, applied throughout this package:

- **"The shelf is verifiably empty" was refuted.** The adjacent AI-parenting shelf is announced and filling — [Sherry Turkle's *Artificial Intimacy* (Little, Brown, Sept 29, 2026)](https://www.hachettebookgroup.com/titles/sherry-turkle/artificial-intimacy/9780316573962/), [Jean Twenge's *10 Rules for Raising Kids in a High-Tech World* (Simon & Schuster, Sept 2025)](https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/10-Rules-for-Raising-Kids-in-a-High-Tech-World/Jean-M-Twenge/9781668099995), [Dana Suskind's *Human Raised* (Dutton, July 14, 2026)](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/811792/human-raised-by-dana-suskind-md/), the AAP's *Connecting* (Moreno & Radesky, Oct 2026), Daniel Susskind's *What Should My Children Do?* (Penguin Press, Nov 2026), and [James Muldoon's *Love Machines* (Faber, Jan 2026)](https://www.faber.co.uk/product/9780571399277-love-machines/) — a wave [Publishers Weekly covered on May 4, 2026](https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/new-titles/adult-announcements/article/100308-new-books-on-kids-and-technology.html). The package's competitive frame is now "crowded adjacent shelf, empty niche": as of July 2026, no traditionally published, book-length, teen-focused, clinician-authored scripts-and-protocols guide dedicated to AI-companion attachment exists or is announced — and every qualifier in that sentence is load-bearing.
- **The headline statistics were rebased to their true definitions.** The tournament-stage "72% of teens already using one" became the honest two-number framing: 72% of teens have *tried* an AI companion at least once under [Common Sense Media's broad, usage-based definition](https://www.commonsensemedia.org/press-releases/nearly-3-in-4-teens-have-used-ai-companions-new-national-survey-finds) (which counts general-purpose chatbots used conversationally), while [Pew's companionship-specific measures](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2026/02/24/how-teens-use-and-view-ai/) find roughly 1 in 8 teens (12%) using a chatbot for emotional support or advice. The "33% chose the bot over you" hook was corrected for base and tense: the 33% figure is among teen *users*, so the citable claim is that [about one in four American teens has, at least once, taken a serious problem to an AI instead of a person](https://x.com/CommonSense/status/1945486943917371578) — a one-time behavior, not a standing preference.
- **The Character.AI change was scoped correctly**: [announced October 29, 2025](https://blog.character.ai/u18-chat-announcement/), with [phased removal of open-ended chat for US minors beginning November 24, 2025](https://techcrunch.com/2025/10/29/character-ai-is-killing-the-chatbot-experience-for-minors/) — an end to open-ended chat for under-18s, not a full platform ban; minors kept non-chat creative features.
- **The post-ban migration claim was demoted from finding to hypothesis** — no published data documents where Character.AI's minor users went — and redesigned as a module of the package's proposed proprietary parent survey.
- **The settlement-leverage claim was corrected as legally wrong.** The [Character.AI/Google settlements (filings public January 7, 2026)](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/07/business/character-ai-google-settle-teen-suicide-lawsuit) are confidential, with no admission of liability; they entitle a nonparty parent to demand nothing. The platform-combat chapter was rebuilt on [California SB 243's](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260SB243) actual provisions — private right of action (greater of actual damages or $1,000 per violation, plus injunctive relief and attorney's fees), mandated and published self-harm crisis protocols, AI-disclosure duties, and break reminders for known minors.
- **Jonathan Haidt was reclassified from friendly predecessor to active, hostile incumbent** (his ["Don't Give Your Child an AI Companion" campaign](https://www.afterbabel.com/p/dont-give-your-child-an-ai-companion) preaches abstinence into the exact PTA channel this book targets), the "DON'T ban your kid's AI girlfriend" counterprogramming hook was killed in favor of "bans alone backfire — this is the working exit ramp to zero," the regulatory contingency was inverted into the primary frame ("the ban just passed — now what?"), the Labor Day 2026 schedule was declared arithmetically dead and reset, and the P&L was re-costed to clinician-protocol comps rather than *Anxious Generation* fantasy.

### Phase 4 — Drafting and Completeness Critique

The package documents were drafted from the adjudicated inputs, under three binding rules: the adjudication overrides the tournament concept wherever they conflict; only the corrected-fact canonical wordings may be used for statistics, each with an inline citation to a real URL; and the competitive frame must name the adjacent-shelf rivals rather than claim emptiness. Each document then received a completeness critique against the adjudication record before finalization.

### The five losing concepts, and why each lost

**The Off-Ramp — GLP-1 discontinuation aftermath (117).** *Life After Ozempic*: a doctor-and-journalist guide to the taper, weight-regain science, returning "food noise," identity aftershocks, and the restart decision, for the tens of millions of Americans on GLP-1 drugs — [1 in 8 US adults currently, per KFF](https://www.kff.org/public-opinion/poll-1-in-8-adults-say-they-are-currently-taking-a-glp-1-drug-for-weight-loss-diabetes-or-another-condition-even-as-half-say-the-drugs-are-difficult-to-afford/) — as [insurers drop coverage](https://www.npr.org/2026/06/23/nx-s1-5793971/weight-loss-glp1-insurance-coverage) and [Cambridge research finds most weight regained within about 18 months of stopping](https://www.cam.ac.uk/research/news/patients-regain-weight-rapidly-after-stopping-weight-loss-drugs-but-still-keep-off-a-quarter-of). It lost on three converging judgments: the window was the fastest-closing in the field (BenBella's [*Losing the Weight Loss Meds*](https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/Losing-the-Weight-Loss-Meds/Holly-R-Wyatt/9781637747803) had already planted the off-ramp flag, and Penguin was seen as one phone call from a platformed-MD maintenance sequel); diet buyers buy hope, not aftermath, so the honest "most of it comes back — defend your 25%" promise loses the thumbnail war to any rival willing to overpromise; and the pharma pipeline (cheap oral GLP-1s, price competition) could shrink the forced-off population between deal and publication date.

**So You Bought a Bible — Gen-Z Christianity on-ramp (111).** *Christianity from zero, for a generation that never went to church*: a culturally fluent, zero-assumed-knowledge, translate-don't-convert companion for the Gen-Z first-time buyers driving [US Bible sales to roughly 19 million units in 2025, a 21-year high](https://religionnews.com/2025/11/17/bible-sales-keep-soaring-even-as-americans-lose-their-religion/), in a religion category that was [one of print's few growth areas](https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/industry-news/financial-reporting/article/99417-print-book-sales-rose-slightly-in-2025.html). Judges called it the freshest thesis on the table ("fluency, not faith" exploits a seam neither Christian houses nor secular imprints can occupy) with the hardest industry data in the set. It lost because curiosity is not crisis — the pain score can't drive a 2 a.m. purchase; because the Bible-buyer-to-explainer-buyer conversion is pure inference (much of the unit surge is premium-binding and replacement purchasing); because the seam positioning risks a book neither B&N nor Christian retail champions; and because the author search — a credible young translator-not-evangelist — is a 6–12 month unicorn hunt sitting directly on the critical path, where a miss produces exactly the apologetics book the concept exists to avoid.

**When the School Bus Stops Coming — autism services cliff (107).** *The '36-Hour Day' for the autism generation's next crisis*: a dated, age-by-age countdown for the families of the [~50,000 autistic youths finishing US high school each year](https://drexel.edu/~/media/Files/autismoutcomes/publications/LCO%20Fact%20Sheet%20Services%20Cliff.ashx) as school-based supports vanish, arriving the year the [ABLE Age Adjustment Act (effective January 1, 2026)](https://www.ablenrc.org/the-able-age-adjustment-act-fact-sheet/) made every competing title's financial guidance legally obsolete. Judges agreed it was in some sense the *best book* in the field — the sharpest, most self-aware pain; a regulatory hook that expires the competition rather than outshouting it; zero preemption risk; the cheapest author search. It lost on ceiling: a niche annuity serving roughly 50,000 families a year, sold through nonprofit and parent-center channels whose free PDFs are simultaneously the sales force and the substitute — a decade-long compounding backlist business at a modest advance, not a Big-5 lead title, and the tournament was scoring for a lead title.

**The Safe Word — AI scam defense (103).** *The Gift of Fear for the deepfake era*: a drillable family defense system — safe words, callback rituals, 24-hour money holds — against AI voice-clone and deepfake fraud targeting older Americans, sold to adult children as the book they hand their parents at Thanksgiving, against a shelf whose category leader predates consumer generative AI entirely and a demand signal that includes [NCOA publishing a web guide in the absence of any current book](https://www.ncoa.org/article/what-are-ai-scams-a-guide-for-older-adults/). It lost on four grounds: scam-defense books are reliable midlist but never breakout, and this package had no celebrity; the free-content substitution problem is fatal at the register — the entire safe-word protocol fits on a wallet card that AARP, NCOA, and every local news segment give away at $0; the headline loss statistics are vendor-sourced (a "1,210% increase" figure traced to a security vendor) and were expected to shred under serious vetting; and the title reads as BDSM at Amazon-thumbnail distance, poisoning the exact gift-purchase mechanic the model depends on. Notably, the acquiring-editor persona still named a retitled version of this concept as their personal acquisition — on the logic that deepfake fraud will be strictly worse in 2028, so its urgency compounds — but the panel's aggregate scoring buried it on pain and commercial ceiling.

**The Parent Emergency — eldercare triage (96).** *A triage guide to the ten phone calls that change your life*: an ER-structured, crisis-sequenced aging-parents field guide with 72-hour checklists and current money rules, replacing a 650-page category bible last revised in 2014, aimed at the [63 million US family caregivers AARP counted in July 2025](https://www.aarp.org/press/releases/2025-07-24-new-report-reveals-crisis-point-for-americas-63-million-family-caregivers.html) and timed to the first boomers turning 80 in 2026. Three scouts independently converged on the underlying opportunity, and judges praised the deepest author bench and demographics ironclad through 2040. It lost on originality and velocity: crisis-sequencing an existing category is a format play, not a new book; "Peak 80" is a manufactured peg on an evergreen shelf; the buyer Googles from the ambulance bay instead of buying books, in a lane publishers quietly know underperforms its market size; discovery runs through [Virginia Morris's 500,000-copy incumbent](https://www.hachettebookgroup.com/titles/virginia-morris/how-to-care-for-aging-parents-3rd-edition/9780761166764/), which Workman could refresh with a fourth edition overnight; and Jennifer Levin's *Generation Care* (2025) already claims the adjacent millennial-caregiver headline.

### Ranked opportunities that never reached the tournament

- **Rank 7 — the post-AI graduate-employment collapse** ([43% of recent graduates underemployed, entry-level postings down sharply](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/09/07/ai-entry-level-jobs-hiring-careers.html)): cross-validated by two scouts with an enormous audience and a built-in gift occasion, but judged the single most media-saturated topic in the set, implying the heaviest invisible pipeline of agented 2027 titles — a race the project could win only on reporting depth, not on being first.
- **Rank 8 — post-dating-app offline romance** ([Match Group paid users down 5% year over year; industry-wide Gen-Z exodus](https://fortune.com/article/are-dating-apps-worth-it-gen-z-millennials-bumble-tinder-hinge/)): original, with corporate-financial rather than discourse-based evidence, but heavily author-platform-dependent in a category where the author *is* the product.

### Notable discarded opportunities from the Phase 1 sweep

The synthesis pass documented why each strong candidate was cut. In condensed form:

1. **AI cognitive atrophy ("Deep Work for the LLM era")** — vivid first-person pain and a real gap, but instantly preemptable by Cal Newport or any established attention author; quotes unverifiable beyond search extracts; the most "obvious next book" in the AI-anxiety space.
2. **AI career-identity grief / mid-career AI survival** (two scouts, merged) — genuine emerging pain, but essay-heavy evidence, two versions pulling in different directions (grief memoir vs. reskilling playbook), and business-press visibility implying a heavy acquisition pipeline; partially represented via the rank-7 opportunity.
3. **Midlife-heroine romantasy** — the best fiction signal in the sweep (editors reportedly aging heroines down is structural supply blockage), but fiction concepts carry 18–24-month timing risk, the key survey dated to 2017, and success depends on a specific novel, not a concept; flagged as the top fiction wildcard.
4. **Low-spice "yearning" secular romance and horrormance/femgore** (three scout entries, two merged) — real reader demand and named editor interest, but publishers visibly already acquiring there: trend-surfing, not gap-filling.
5. **Young men not reading fiction** (two scouts converged) — convergence was on industry discourse rather than reader pain; key statistics contested; fatal flaw that the target buyer under-buys books by definition.
6. **Male friendship recession** — strong verified statistics but saturated "male loneliness" discourse, a thin shelf scan, and a gap claim resting on absence of evidence.
7. **Gen-Z brain rot / analog living** — a good retail timing signal, but the digital-wellness shelf is the most crowded adjacent category in nonfiction and voice-only differentiation is too weak a moat.
8. **Solo agers** — AARP itself notes the shelf is expanding; only a quality/currency gap.
9. **Post-Surfside HOA/condo boards** — specific and urgent but Florida-concentrated, with unproven individual book-buying; a niche-press play.
10. **Boomer business exit** — a relative gap in a category already saturated with advisor-funnel books.
11. **Fractional-executive playbook** — demand statistics come from marketplaces incentivized to hype; works only as an author bet, not a concept bet.

The sweep's dead-end log also records topics investigated and dropped before ranking, including perimenopause (recently well served), Gen-Z BNPL debt (crowded shelf), parent estrangement (well covered since 2021), adult AI-partner grief as a standalone book (vivid pain, but Muldoon's *Love Machines* already occupies the space and the buying audience is unproven), and teacher-side AI cheating (institutional rather than consumer pain; partly folded into the winning parenting opportunity).

## 3. Research Constraints, Honestly Disclosed

**The central constraint: search extracts, not full-page reads.** The research environment's egress proxy policy-denied nearly every full-page fetch attempted during the Phase 1 sweep — WebFetch and curl received 403 CONNECT rejections for Reddit, arXiv, Hacker News, CNBC, MIT Technology Review, Fortune, NPR, Amazon, Goodreads, AARP, Common Sense Media, publisher catalog pages, and most news domains, and the fallback remote-browser tool required an approval unavailable to the research processes. Every scout independently logged this. Consequently, **the evidence base rests on substantive search-result extracts — which returned dated, often verbatim page content — tied to real URLs surfaced in those searches, not on full-page reads of the cited pages.** Figures that recurred across multiple independent search results (the Common Sense Media 72%/52%/33% suite, print-category Circana toplines, the 19M Bible-unit figure, the 2M+ *Anxious Generation* sales claim) are the most reliable; single-extract figures are the least. The Phase 3 fact-check pass mitigated this substantially for the winning concept — it corrected dates, bases, definitions, scopes, and two outright refuted claims, and produced canonical wordings with primary-source URLs — but it operated under the same fetch constraints.

**Additional constraints on the record:**

- **Temporal cutoff.** All research was conducted in early July 2026 (adjudication dated July 9, 2026). Anything announced afterward is invisible to this package.
- **Pipeline invisibility.** Unannounced book deals cannot be searched. The preemption skeptic exhaustively searched Publishers Marketplace deal coverage, PW previews, and every named natural author and found no announced rival in the clinician-protocol lane — but a negative cannot be proven, and the adjudication treats Turkle's September 29, 2026 launch as the start of the me-too clock.
- **Vendor-sourced and secondary statistics.** Some figures in the *losing* concepts (e.g., the scam-defense concept's 1,210% vendor statistic; several second-hand extracts in the eldercare and dating entries) were flagged by scouts and judges as needing verification and were never subjected to the Phase 3 fact-check, which covered the winner only. They are reproduced in Section 2 solely as tournament record.
- **Archived-record artifact.** As noted in Section 2, the tournament score table's narrative rationale texts are cross-attributed between rows in the archived files; the numeric scores are internally consistent, but prose attribution was reconstructed by content.
- **Derived figures.** The ~42M US children aged 8–17 addressable base is a derived approximation from [Census age-bucket data](https://www.childstats.gov/americaschildren/tables/pop1.asp), not a published figure.
