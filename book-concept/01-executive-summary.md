# Executive Summary

## Your Kid's AI Best Friend
### What to Do When the Phone Loves Them Back — Scripts, House Rules, and the Exit Ramp From a Teen's AI Companion

**One-line pitch:** *The Anxious Generation* told parents the phone stole their kid's childhood; this is the first clinician's protocol book for the parents who just discovered the phone now loves their kid back — word-for-word scripts, a one-page Family AI Agreement, and a step-by-step Breakup protocol for detecting, defusing, and out-relating a teen's attachment to an AI companion, at the exact moment the first wave of under-18 bans is proving that a law can shut off an app but cannot un-attach a kid.

**Category and format:** Family & Relationships / Parenting — Teenagers (FAM039000). Prescriptive nonfiction with a reported case-study spine and a crisis-triage front section, in the *Good Inside* / *How to Talk So Kids Will Listen* lineage. Format hedged below hardcover-only economics (trade paperback or simultaneous paperback), with the P&L sized to clinician-protocol comps such as *Good Inside* — not to *Anxious Generation* fantasy numbers.

## The reader problem — with honest numbers

A relationship has moved into American teenagers' phones, and most parents have not met it. In Common Sense Media's nationally representative NORC survey (n=1,060 teens 13–17, July 2025), [72% of teens said they had used an AI companion at least once](https://www.commonsensemedia.org/press-releases/nearly-3-in-4-teens-have-used-ai-companions-new-national-survey-finds) — under a deliberately broad definition that counts general-purpose chatbots like ChatGPT used for personal conversation — and 52% use one at least a few times a month. Companionship-specific measures run lower and are the honest core of the case: [Pew Research](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2026/02/24/how-teens-use-and-view-ai/) (n=1,458 parent-teen pairs, published February 2026) finds roughly **1 in 8 teens using a chatbot for emotional support or advice — about 3 million American kids** — and a peer-reviewed FAU/UW study ([*Journal of Adolescence*, May 2026, n=3,466](https://www.fau.edu/newsdesk/articles/chatbot-ai-study-teens)) finds 60.2% have ever tried one, 11.4% use one near-daily, and 47.1% of users have experienced at least one of thirteen measured risks. The book leads with the convergent, deflation-proof headline: **[one in four American teens has, at least once, taken a serious problem to an AI instead of a person](https://www.commonsensemedia.org/sites/default/files/research/report/talk-trust-and-trade-offs_2025_web.pdf)**.

The parents are not calm about this and they are not equipped. Emotional support from a chatbot is [the only teen AI use a majority of parents oppose — just 18% are comfortable with it](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2026/02/24/what-parents-say-about-their-teens-ai-use/) — yet roughly 4 in 10 parents have never discussed chatbots with their teen, and parents systematically underestimate use. The risk is documented capability, not speculation: in the [Common Sense Media risk assessment conducted with Stanford Brainstorm clinicians (April 2025)](https://www.commonsensemedia.org/press-releases/ai-companions-decoded-common-sense-media-recommends-ai-companion-safety-standards), testers posing as teens readily elicited sexual role-play and self-harm encouragement, producing an "unacceptable risk" verdict for under-18s. A [peer-reviewed Drexel-led study (CHI 2026)](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3772318.3790597) found teens describing their own companion use in the language of all six components of behavioral addiction — withdrawal, tolerance, relapse. And the calibration that keeps this book out of moral-panic territory comes from the same sources: [80% of teen users still spend more time with real friends than with bots](https://www.commonsensemedia.org/sites/default/files/research/report/talk-trust-and-trade-offs_2025_web.pdf), and about half view companions as tools, not friends.

**The buyer:** mothers 35–55 of kids 10–17 — the demographic behind *The Anxious Generation*'s [two-million-plus copies and 111 consecutive weeks on the NYT list](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/729231/the-anxious-generation-by-jonathan-haidt/) — served in two states: the crisis buyer who just found the chat logs (a 72-hour triage section opens the book) and the anxious-but-unaware buyer (reached by the Attachment Check-In, a free viral quiz that manufactures the discovery moment). Secondary: school counselors, therapists, and PTAs — the proven bulk channel — with chapters written to be assigned across a household, making two-plus copies the natural unit of sale. Addressable base: parents of the [~42 million US kids aged 8–17](https://www.childstats.gov/americaschildren/tables/pop1.asp).

## Why now: the 2026–2027 catalyst calendar

The governing frame is **"the ban just passed — now what?"** On October 29, 2025, [Character.AI announced the removal of open-ended chat for under-18s](https://blog.character.ai/u18-chat-announcement/), phased in from November 24, 2025 — not a platform ban; minors kept non-chat features — and [its own CEO predicted teens might simply flock to other platforms](https://techcrunch.com/2025/10/29/character-ai-is-killing-the-chatbot-experience-for-minors/). In [filings made public January 7, 2026, Character.AI and Google agreed to settle five teen-suicide and mental-health lawsuits](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/07/business/character-ai-google-settle-teen-suicide-lawsuit) — confidential, no admission of liability. [California SB 243](https://sd18.senate.ca.gov/news/first-nation-ai-chatbot-safeguards-signed-law), operative January 1, 2026, created the first companion-chatbot regime, including a [private right of action worth the greater of actual damages or $1,000 per violation](https://natlawreview.com/article/when-ai-feels-human-californias-sb-243-opens-door-private-lawsuits). The [federal GUARD Act advanced out of Senate Judiciary unanimously on April 30, 2026](https://rollcall.com/2026/04/30/ban-on-kids-companion-chatbots-advanced-by-senate-committee/); [New York enacted an under-18 ban in June 2026 with fines up to $25,000 per violation](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/318339/20260613/new-york-bans-ai-companion-chatbots-kids-unanimous-vote-sets-25000-fines.htm), with Idaho, Oregon, Washington, and Nebraska [following](https://www.multistate.us/insider/2026/4/30/state-childrens-online-safety-laws-expand-beyond-social-media-in-2026). MIT Technology Review named AI companions a [2026 Breakthrough Technology](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/01/12/1130018/ai-companions-chatbots-relationships-2026-breakthrough-technology/). Forward pegs are dateable through the pub window: the GUARD Act floor fight, NY enforcement, [first SB 243 operator safety reports due July 1, 2027](https://www.gunder.com/en/news-insights/insights/client-insight-california-sb-243-new-compliance-requirements-for-operators-of-ai-companion-chatbots), expanding litigation against OpenAI, and Common Sense Media's annual teen-AI census. Every legislative win amplifies rather than solves the parent's problem — the law shuts off an app; it does not un-attach a kid, detect the migration to a shadow platform, or script Tuesday night's conversation. Precedent says legislation sells parent books: *The Anxious Generation* sold through the entire state phone-ban wave.

## The competitive frame: crowded adjacent shelf, empty niche

The AI-parenting shelf is announced and filling — [Publishers Weekly covered the wave in May 2026](https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/new-titles/adult-announcements/article/100308-new-books-on-kids-and-technology.html) — and this package names every occupant: Sherry Turkle's [*Artificial Intimacy*](https://www.hachettebookgroup.com/titles/sherry-turkle/artificial-intimacy/9780316573962/) (Little, Brown, Sept 29, 2026) is diagnosis, not protocol; Jean Twenge's [*10 Rules for Raising Kids in a High-Tech World*](https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/10-Rules-for-Raising-Kids-in-a-High-Tech-World/Jean-M-Twenge/9781668099995) (S&S, Sept 2025) gives companions one rule inside a general framework; Dana Suskind's [*Human Raised*](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/811792/human-raised-by-dana-suskind-md/) (Dutton, July 2026) skews early-childhood; the AAP's *Connecting* (Moreno & Radesky, Oct 2026) is general digital parenting; Daniel Susskind's *What Should My Children Do?* (Penguin Press, Nov 2026) is a futures argument; James Muldoon's [*Love Machines*](https://www.faber.co.uk/product/9780571399277-love-machines/) (Faber, Jan 2026) is adult-focused analysis. The defensible claim — which survived an adversarial preemption search of deal coverage, catalogs, and every named natural author: **as of July 2026, no traditionally published, book-length, teen-focused, clinician-authored scripts-and-protocols guide dedicated to AI-companion attachment exists or is announced.** The positioning line writes itself: *Turkle tells you what these machines are doing to your kid; this book tells you what to do Tuesday night.* Free institutional guides from [JED](https://jedfoundation.org/why-your-teen-shouldnt-be-using-ai-companions-and-what-to-do-if-they-are/) and the [APA](https://www.apa.org/topics/artificial-intelligence-machine-learning/teens-chatbots-parents) are cited as the demand signal and as consensus provenance for the protocols; the deliverables they do not and cannot match are script-level depth, the reported case-study spine, age-tiered negotiated Family AI Agreements, and the SB 243 platform-combat chapter with model complaint language. Jonathan Haidt is treated as an active incumbent, not a friendly predecessor: he runs an explicit ["Don't Give Your Child Any AI Companions" campaign](https://www.afterbabel.com/p/dont-give-your-child-an-ai-companion) into the same PTA channel. This book owns the question his abstinence position cannot answer — *what if the ban fails, or your kid is already attached?* — and its thesis ("bans alone backfire; this is the working exit ramp to zero") is compatible enough with the institutional endpoint that Common Sense Media, Child Mind, and Haidt's own machinery are courtable as channel, foreword, and endorsement rather than opposition.

## What the adversarial review concluded

The concept was put through a formal adversarial adjudication — independent skeptics attacking preemption, demand, durability, evidence quality, execution, and legal exposure — and **survived, with eleven mandatory revisions applied throughout this package.** The niche is real; the original framing around it was not. Every "empty shelf" claim is deleted and replaced with the named-competitor frame above; every headline statistic is rebased on its true definition and base (the two-number try-rate/companionship story; "one in four, at least once"); the post-ban migration claim is demoted to a hypothesis to be tested in the book's commissioned 2,000-parent survey; the platform-combat chapter is rebuilt on SB 243's real private right of action, with the confidential, no-admission settlements cited only as evidence that litigation pressure is real; the counterprogramming hook that contradicted every named author candidate's public position is killed in favor of the "exit ramp to zero" thesis; the schedule is reset honestly (author-first, proposal out when signed, realistically Q4 2026, with Turkle's launch as the me-too clock and a crashed spring-2027 pub evaluated against the default 2027–28 window). Seven kill scenarios were prosecuted and failed, including the announced-rival kill, the elite-panic kill (parental concern is [demonstrably cross-partisan and mass-market](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/ai-teens-parents-pew-survey/)), the fad-decay kill, and the legal-evisceration kill.

## Headline risks

1. **The author gate (fatal if unresolved, by design):** no circulation without a signed author. The primary track is a working clinician + tech-accountability journalist pairing, with the load-bearing structure (named protocols, scripts, the Agreement, triage) compensating for a non-celebrity byline; Damour-class names convert to foreword and endorsement.
2. **The Turkle-triggered me-too clock (high/high):** her September 29, 2026 launch will spawn rival proposals; speed and the pre-funded proprietary survey are the defense.
3. **Stat deflation on air (high if unfixed; low after revision):** all marketing is already rebased on the conservative, convergent numbers.
4. **Haidt annexes the category (high/medium):** mitigated by riding his machinery and owning the post-ban question.
5. **Regulatory "solved it" perception and the unproven trade-up from free guides (medium):** answered by the enforcement-gap frame, a pre-proposal quiz-funnel conversion test, and format hedging.
