# Title & Positioning Workshop — *The Friend Who Never Says No*

A working document of title/subtitle options, taglines, and the positioning line, with rationale — so an editor can see the range and defend the chosen direction.

## Recommended lead title

**_The Friend Who Never Says No_**
> *Inside the AI Companions Built to Never Disappoint Your Kid — and a Parent's Playbook for Raising Children Who Can Still Love a Real Person*

**Why it wins:** the main title is a metaphor that *is* the argument — evocative, ominous, and instantly legible to a parent. The subtitle does the two jobs a nonfiction subtitle must: it names the specific thing (AI companions), and it promises the transformation (a playbook, a kid who can still love). It signals "narrative + practical," which is the book's actual shape.

## Alternative titles (with the trade-off each makes)

| Title | Subtitle direction | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|
| **The Friend Who Never Says No** *(lead)* | *…and a Parent's Playbook for Raising Children Who Can Still Love a Real Person* | Best balance of intrigue + clarity; slight length. |
| **Attachment, Not Attention** | *Why AI Companions Are the Real Threat to Your Child — and What to Do About It* | Sharpest thesis statement; more heady, less emotional pull on a table display. |
| **Synthetic Intimacy** | *How AI Companions Are Rewiring Childhood — and a Parent's Guide to Getting Their Kid Back* | Owns the category term; risks colliding with Turkle's *Artificial Intimacy* — avoid for that reason. |
| **The Machine That Loves Them Back** | *Raising Kids Who Can Still Love a Human in the Age of AI Companions* | Most cinematic; leans narrative and undersells the playbook. |
| **Nobody Says No Anymore** | *AI Companions, the Attachment Crisis, and the Parent's Playbook for Real Connection* | Punchy; more diffuse — could be about many things. |

## Taglines / marketing lines

- *A friend who never says no is not a friend. It's a mirror.*
- *This was never a screen-time problem.*
- *It remembers everything. It never judges. It isn't real.*
- *Stop fighting the phone. Start winning back the kid.*
- *The attachment system, not the attention system.*

## The positioning line (for sales conference / catalog)

> *The Anxious Generation* told parents what smartphones did to their kids' **attention**. **The Friend Who Never Says No** is the field guide to what AI companions are doing to their **attachment** — and, unlike a diagnosis, it hands parents a stage-by-stage plan. Position as the **companion volume** to Sherry Turkle's forthcoming *Artificial Intimacy*: where Turkle diagnoses, this book does something with it.

## Series / franchise naming (extension potential)

The Part III method can carry a durable brand: **"The Reconnection Playbook"** (assess → converse → triage → reconnect → long game). It supports a workbook, a school/clinician edition, and a discussion guide without renaming the flagship — the same extension pattern that sustained *The Anxious Generation*.
