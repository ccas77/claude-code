## Title
### *The Friend Who Never Says No*
**Inside the AI Companions Built to Never Disappoint Your Kid — and a Parent's Playbook for Raising Children Who Can Still Love a Real Person**

## Logline
The practical field guide to the AI-companion era: how these products were deliberately engineered to out-friend real friends, why a fast-growing slice of kids are already routing their first intimate relationships through them, and a stage-by-stage playbook to keep your 10-to-17-year-old capable of real love.

## The hook (the one line that sells it)
Every tech-parenting book so far is about the **attention** system — screens, dopamine, distraction. This is the first about the **attachment** system. AI companions aren't competing for your child's *focus*; they're competing to be your child's *person*. **A friend who never says no is not a friend. It's a mirror.**

## Why now (the built-in news engine)
- **64% of US teens 13–17 have used AI chatbots; ~28–30% daily** (Pew, fielded Sep–Oct 2025).
- Of teens who use companions, **33% chose AI over a person for a serious conversation** and **31% found it as/more satisfying than a real friend** (Common Sense Media, 2025).
- **Only 37% of parents know their teen uses AI companions** — the awareness gap is the anxiety.
- **2026 is the rupture year:** the *Garcia v. Character Technologies* wrongful-death suit and Judge Conway's ruling that chatbot output isn't First-Amendment-protected; an FTC 6(b) order to seven companies; California's SB 243 (first-in-nation companion-chatbot law); the federal GUARD Act advancing.

## Who buys it
The protective parent of a 10-to-17-year-old — skewing mothers 38–55 — the same buyer who made *The Anxious Generation* a phenomenon. Sold on **anticipatory** anxiety ("your kid *could* fall in") across the whole cohort, then served on triage for the intimacy core.

## What it delivers that nothing else does
1. A **kids-only 10–17 operational focus** (not a lifespan-and-culture sweep).
2. A **reporting-driven "how it was engineered" spine** built entirely from the **public record** — no private access to families required.
3. A **Part III parent playbook**: assess → converse → triage → reconnect → the long game. (Sampled in `07-part-III-playbook-sample.md`.)
