# COMPLETENESS CRITIC — FINAL REVIEW

## DoD Requirement Scorecard

**R1 — "A stranger can read the concept package and understand the book": MET.**
The concept dossier + proposal + intro excerpt make the thesis, reframe, audience, and structure fully legible. The "friend who never says no / attachment not attention" idea lands cleanly in every document. No stranger would be confused about *what the book is*.

**R2 — "…review the pitch materials": PARTIAL.**
A proposal, comps, marketing plan, chapter outline, and intro excerpt exist. But the pitch's single most important asset for a real acquisition — a sample of the **Part III playbook** — is entirely absent (see Gap 2). The materials pitch a product whose differentiating component is never shown.

**R3 — "…understand the market evidence": PARTIAL.**
The market dossier is genuinely strong on demand signals and admirably honest about soft data. But it **explicitly declines to provide a TAM headcount** ("deliberately not asserted… should be added at proposal stage"), provides **no P&L / price / advance / royalty model** despite repeatedly invoking "underwrite to 75k–250k," and rests on ~40 external hyperlinks, several **flagged as 403/inaccessible and only "corroborated."** A stranger cannot size the market or model the economics from the folder alone.

**R4 — "…fully evaluate the concept without external assistance": NOT MET.**
Two blockers. (a) The package itself names *platform transfer* as "the entire commercial risk," then attaches **no author, no shortlist, no candidate, no acquisition path** — so the deciding variable is unevaluable by design. (b) The evidence base is cited by link, not reproduced; verifying it *requires* leaving the folder, and some links are noted as dead. "Without external assistance" fails on both counts.

**R5 — "Every fact/stat/claim verified and cited, no inventing": PARTIAL.**
Most stats are diligently cited and definitions disclosed (the 72% vs 12–16% handling is exemplary). But several load-bearing claims are uncited or unverifiable (see Factual Risks 1, 2, 6, 7).

**R6 — "Strongest / most original / most commercially promising defensible option — NOT the safest": NOT MET (as demonstrated).**
This is the requirement the package most clearly fails to *evidence*. The concept is explicitly and repeatedly positioned as the **safe, subordinate play**: "we do **not** try to out-diagnose," "the **companion playbook** to her diagnosis," "field guide to Turkle," "underwrite to a fraction," conservative posture throughout. Its one original move — "attachment, not attention" — is openly **credited to the Center for Humane Technology and FEPS, not to the book**. Most importantly, **no set of rejected bolder alternatives is shown**, so there is zero evidence this was *selected as* the strongest/most original from a field. It reads as the most *defensible* option rebranded as candor — which is the opposite of the brief's explicit "not the safest" instruction.

## Concrete Gaps a Stranger Hits

1. **No author, no platform path — the deciding variable is blank.** Every document declares a platform author a "build precondition" and names transfer as *the* commercial risk, then offers no candidate, profile shortlist, or plan to secure one. A stranger asked "greenlight?" cannot answer, because the package tells them the answer depends entirely on a thing it doesn't supply.

2. **The differentiator (Part III) is never sampled.** The entire standalone-vs-free-toolkit argument rests on the playbook being deeper than a PDF. Not one assessment item, triage rule, or reconnection script is shown — only *descriptions* of them. The reader must take the book's core value on faith.

3. **Chapter structure is internally inconsistent across three documents (hard contradiction).**
   - Proposal §7 + §8: **10 chapters + intro**, Parts titled "How the Friend Was Built / Why It Matters / Getting Your Kid Back."
   - toc-sample: **14 chapters**, Parts titled "The Seduction / Why It Matters / The Playbook."
   - concept-package §9: Parts titled "The Seduction / The Reason to Worry / The Playbook."
   Chapter titles and mappings differ (e.g., Proposal Ch.1 "Engineered to Out-Friend Your Friends" vs. toc Ch.1 "The Friend Who Never Says No"). A stranger cannot tell how many chapters the book has or what the parts are called.

4. **No market size number.** By the dossier's own admission there is no TAM headcount and no category dollar figure it will stand behind.

5. **No economics.** "Underwrite to 75k–250k" is asserted in four places; no P&L, list price, print run, or advance range is ever modeled.

6. **No navigation/README and heavy triplication.** The risks section appears three times near-verbatim across documents; there is no index telling a stranger what each file is or the canonical version of contested facts (e.g., is awareness 37% or 51%?).

## Factual Risks (unsourced / overstated / inconsistent)

1. **Claims about Turkle's *unpublished* book stated as verified fact.** *Artificial Intimacy* doesn't publish until Sept 29, 2026 (2.5 months after this dossier's date), yet the package repeatedly asserts it has "the **identical** attachment framing, childhood-to-parenthood scope, and best-friend/lover/therapist taxonomy." This is the linchpin of the entire "companion volume" positioning and it is **unverifiable** — likely inferred from a catalog blurb, but presented as known interior content. Overstated and load-bearing.

2. **"Concentrated among the loneliest and most vulnerable" presented as fact.** Proposal Overview and toc Ch.6 characterize the 12% intimacy-use slice as concentrated in vulnerable kids. Pew's 12% is a use-case measure; the demographic concentration is an *inference* (partly supported by reverse-causation literature) but is stated as established. Unsourced causal-demographic claim.

3. **"Endorsed as a concern by the APA" — no citation.** Concept §3 credits an APA endorsement of the framing with no link, while carefully citing everything around it.

4. **"FEPS 'from attention to attachment' work" — no citation.** Named twice (concept §3, toc Ch.6) as support; never linked.

5. **Two distinct OpenAI 0.15% metrics conflated.** Concept §5/§7 phrase it as "~0.15%… show heightened emotional attachment **and** over a million a week discuss suicide," implying one figure. Per the market dossier these are *separate* 0.15% populations (attachment vs. suicidal-ideation). The intimacy-attachment stat and the suicide stat are being fused into a single scarier sentence.

6. **JAMA Pediatrics cited as a 2025 source it admits is unpublished.** Concept §5 cites "JAMA Pediatrics / RAND, 2025" for the secrecy figure; the market dossier says the JAMA Pediatrics paper is still *pending* and the "1-in-5" vs "1-in-8" numbers are "unreconciled pending" it. The concept package cites a paper the evidence base flags as not yet out.

7. **Intro's dramatized specifics without source.** The excerpt opens "At 8:30 on the last night of his life…" — a precise factual detail about Sewell Setzer's final evening presented with no citation (the closing source note covers stats, not this narrative particular). Either sourced to the complaint (then cite it) or it's invented color in a package whose whole credibility pitch is "no inventing."

8. **Awareness figure ambiguity (37% vs 51%).** Both appear as "the awareness gap." They measure different things (companion awareness via Torney/Common Sense vs. chatbot awareness via Pew), and the docs *mostly* keep them straight — but §7 of the concept package stacks them adjacently in a way a stranger will read as contradictory. Needs one reconciling sentence, present in the market doc but not the concept doc.

9. **Character.AI ban cited to two different URLs** across documents (`/u18-chat-announcement/` vs `/an-update-on-changes-to-our-under-18-experience/`), and "multi-year bestseller run" (concept §4) overstates the 73-week (~1.4-year) *Anxious Generation* figure the same package cites.

## Overall Verdict

The package is intellectually sharp, unusually honest about weak data, and its prose is publishable. But against *this* DoD it has three disqualifying holes: (1) it fails the "not the safest" mandate on its own stated terms and shows no evidence of having been selected as the strongest from bolder alternatives; (2) it cannot be "fully evaluated" because the variable it declares decisive — the platform author — is absent, and the differentiating Part III is never sampled; (3) the chapter architecture contradicts itself across three documents, and several load-bearing claims (Turkle's unpublished contents, APA/FEPS endorsements, the JAMA citation, the dramatized death detail) are unsourced or unverifiable in a package whose central promise is "no inventing."

**READY TO SHIP: false.**

Minimum to flip to true: attach an author/platform plan (or reframe the deliverable as author-search-first); add at least one real sample instrument from Part III; reconcile the 10-vs-14-chapter structure and the three sets of part titles to one canonical outline; downgrade every claim about Turkle's unpublished book to "per publisher listing, apparently"; source or cut the APA endorsement, the FEPS reference, the JAMA-Pediatrics citation, and the "8:30 last night" detail; and either supply a TAM headcount + skeleton P&L or stop asserting "underwrite to 75k–250k" as if modeled.
