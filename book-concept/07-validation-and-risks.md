# Validation Report and Risk Register

**Concept:** *Your Kid's AI Best Friend* — a clinician-authored, scripts-and-protocols parenting guide to teen AI-companion attachment
**Document status:** Final validation summary, July 2026
**Bottom line:** The concept **survived adversarial review and is cleared to proceed — but only in its revised form.** Three of its original claims were refuted outright and have been corrected throughout this package. The niche is real; the original framing around it was false. What follows is the full record of what was attacked, what broke, what was fixed, and what conditions must still be met before anyone spends money on this.

---

## 1. How the Concept Was Stress-Tested

Before this package was finalized, the tournament-winning concept was handed to a structured adversarial review whose explicit brief was to kill it. The process had three layers:

**Five skeptic kill-angles, 28 attacks.** Five independent skeptics each prosecuted a distinct way the book could fail:

| Kill-angle | Question prosecuted | Attacks filed | Refuted | Weakened | Survived |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **Preemption** | Is a rival book already announced? Is the shelf really empty? | 5 | 2 | 2 | 1 |
| **Demand** | Do median parents actually care, and does this topic convert to book purchases? | 5 | 0 | 3 | 2 |
| **Evidence** | Do the headline statistics and legal claims survive a hostile fact-check? | 7 | 0 | 5 | 2 |
| **Durability** | Is this a fad — a "Pokémon Go parenting guide" — or will regulation solve it before pub date? | 5 | 0 | 2 | 3 |
| **Execution** | Can the author be signed, the schedule met, and the protocols defended? | 6 | 1 | 4 | 1 |
| **Total** | | **28** | **3** | **16** | **9** |

A verdict of *refuted* means the skeptic proved a proposal claim false; *weakened* means the claim survives only in a corrected or narrowed form; *survived* means the attack itself failed — the skeptic prosecuted the charge and could not make it stick.

**Six fact-check clusters, 21 checks.** In parallel, fact-checkers verified every load-bearing factual claim in the proposal against primary sources: the Common Sense Media survey statistics (5 checks), the platform and regulatory dates (2), the settlements and the Common Sense "unacceptable risk" verdict (2), the Stanford risk assessment and the arXiv addiction-language study (3), the Haidt comp claims and market-size figures (5), and the competitive-shelf claims (4). Result: **15 confirmed, 6 rated partly-true and corrected.** No load-bearing claim was found to be fabricated; six were found to be deployed with the wrong date, base, definition, or superlative, and each now carries a mandated canonical wording (Section 3).

**Adjudication.** An adjudicator weighed the attacks and checks together and issued a binding output: a proceed decision, 11 mandatory concept revisions (all applied across this package), 17 corrected facts with canonical citable wordings, a 10-item risk register, and a record of the kill scenarios that failed. The pattern across all five skeptics was strikingly consistent — each concluded some version of "do not proceed as written, but the kill shot does not exist." The preemption skeptic's own summary is the epigraph for the whole exercise: **"the niche is real; the framing is false."**

## 2. The Three Refuted Claims — and the Revisions They Forced

Three claims in the original proposal were proven false. Each refutation forced a structural revision, and in each case the honest replacement is stronger material than the claim it replaced.

### 2.1 "The shelf is verifiably empty" — refuted by catalog pages

The original differentiation section claimed "the shelf is verifiably empty" and that "a July 2026 preemption check confirms no announced mainstream trade book." The preemption skeptic refuted both claims with publisher catalog pages, landing two separate refuted verdicts. The most dangerous miss: **Sherry Turkle's *Artificial Intimacy: Who We Become When We Talk to Machines*** ([Little, Brown, September 29, 2026](https://www.hachettebookgroup.com/titles/sherry-turkle/artificial-intimacy/9780316573962/)) — a clinician-authored, Big-5 book that traces chatbot attachment from childhood and cites the same 70-percent-plus teen statistic this concept is built on. Around it, an announced wave: Jean Twenge's *10 Rules for Raising Kids in a High-Tech World* ([Simon & Schuster, Sept 2025](https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/10-Rules-for-Raising-Kids-in-a-High-Tech-World/Jean-M-Twenge/9781668099995)), Dana Suskind's *Human Raised* ([Dutton, July 14, 2026](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/811792/human-raised-by-dana-suskind-md/)), the AAP's *Connecting* (Moreno & Radesky, Oct 2026), Daniel Susskind's *What Should My Children Do?* (Penguin Press, Nov 2026), and James Muldoon's *Love Machines* ([Faber, Jan 2026](https://www.faber.co.uk/product/9780571399277-love-machines/)) — a wave [Publishers Weekly covered on May 4, 2026](https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/new-titles/adult-announcements/article/100308-new-books-on-kids-and-technology.html). As the adjudicator put it, any acquiring editor who read the PW roundup "bounces the proposal at the words 'verifiably empty.'"

**The revision.** The competitive frame across this package is now **"crowded adjacent shelf, empty niche."** Every adjacent title is named, and the surviving claim is deliberately narrow: as of July 2026, after exhaustive search of [Publishers Marketplace deal coverage](https://lunch.publishersmarketplace.com/category/ai/), PW previews, and every natural author's channel, **no traditionally published, book-length, teen-focused, clinician-authored scripts-and-protocols guide dedicated to AI-companion attachment exists or is announced** — with the explicit caveat that every qualifier in that sentence is load-bearing and unannounced deals remain invisible. The same skeptic who destroyed the empty-shelf rhetoric prosecuted this narrow claim and recorded it as the one that survives: "I tried hard to find the announced killer… Nothing surfaced." The positioning now argues against Turkle by name rather than against a hypothetical: *she tells you what these machines are doing to your kid; this book tells you what to do Tuesday night.*

### 2.2 "Three deliverables a liability-averse nonprofit will never publish" — refuted by name and URL

The original free-content defense claimed the web guides could never publish the Breakup protocol, the platform-combat playbook, or the 72-hour triage section. The execution skeptic refuted this "for two of the three deliverables, today, by name": the [JED Foundation](https://jedfoundation.org/why-your-teen-shouldnt-be-using-ai-companions-and-what-to-do-if-they-are/) ("Why Your Teen Shouldn't Be Using AI Companions – and What to Do If They Are") and the [APA](https://www.apa.org/topics/artificial-intelligence-machine-learning/teens-chatbots-parents) ("Your teen turned to AI instead of you") already publish, free, the operative core of both the Breakup protocol (taper not cold-turkey, conversation-first, replacement activities, escalation triggers) and the triage content (what to do tonight, what not to say, red flags, crisis referral). In the skeptic's words: "The liability-averse nonprofits are demonstrably NOT too liability-averse to publish 'what to do if they are' crisis guidance — they are publishing it faster than any hardcover can ship."

**The revision.** The moat has been rebased on what genuinely survives: **script-level depth** (the Faber/Mazlish DNA), **the reported case-study spine**, **age-tiered negotiated Family AI Agreements**, and — the one deliverable absent from every free guide — **the platform-combat playbook built on California SB 243's private right of action**, with model complaint language. The JED and APA guides are now cited on the jacket as the demand signal and as consensus provenance for the protocols, not as a moat the jacket denies.

### 2.3 The settlement-leverage error — legally wrong, and corrected

The original Chapter 9 promised parents "what SB 243 and the settlements entitle you to demand from platforms." The evidence skeptic showed the settlement half of that sentence is legally false: the Character.AI/Google settlements ([made public January 7, 2026](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/07/business/character-ai-google-settle-teen-suicide-lawsuit), anchored by *Garcia v. Character Technologies*, the Sewell Setzer III case) are **confidential, with no admission of liability and no disclosed injunctive relief** ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/07/google-characterai-to-settle-suits-involving-suicides-ai-chatbots.html), [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2026/01/07/google-character-settle-lawsuits-suicide/)). "A confidential settlement of someone else's lawsuit entitles a nonparty parent to demand nothing from any platform, and a trade book telling parents otherwise invites both expert ridicule and publisher legal review." (For the record: the attack ledger scored this one *weakened* rather than *refuted*, because the SB 243 half of the same chapter checked out completely — but the settlement-entitlement claim itself was falsified outright, and the adjudicator treated it as the third claim the process killed.)

**The revision.** Chapter 9's playbook is rebuilt entirely on [California SB 243](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260SB243) — which is better material: a real private right of action (the greater of actual damages or $1,000 per violation, plus injunctive relief and attorney's fees), mandated and published self-harm crisis protocols, AI-disclosure duties, three-hourly break reminders for known minors, and first annual operator reports due to California's Office of Suicide Prevention on July 1, 2027 ([FPF analysis](https://fpf.org/blog/understanding-the-new-wave-of-chatbot-legislation-california-sb-243-and-beyond/), [National Law Review](https://natlawreview.com/article/when-ai-feels-human-californias-sb-243-opens-door-private-lawsuits)). The settlements are cited only as narrative why-now evidence that litigation pressure is real; an honest "what if you're not in California" section covers the state copycats and pending federal law; and a legal reviewer is budgeted into the proposal (see Section 7).

## 5. Kill Scenarios Survived — and Why

Seven distinct kill scenarios were prosecuted to conclusion and failed. These are the concept's strongest validation evidence, because in each case a motivated skeptic with full research access tried to make the scenario land and could not.

1. **The announced-rival kill (preemption).** The skeptic surfaced Turkle, Twenge, Suskind, the AAP, and Susskind — enough to destroy the "empty shelf" rhetoric — but after exhaustively searching [Publishers Marketplace/Lunch deal coverage](https://lunch.publishersmarketplace.com/category/ai/), PW previews, and every named natural author (Heitner, Weinstein, Steyer, Torney, Jordan, Segall), found no announced trade book occupying the clinician-protocol lane on teen AI-companion attachment. The niche survives because Turkle is diagnosis, Twenge gives it one rule, Suskind skews early-childhood, and the AAP is general digital parenting. The skeptic's own verdict: "the niche is real; the framing is false."

2. **The elite-panic kill (demand).** The attempt to show median parents don't care failed on [Pew's own data](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2026/02/24/what-parents-say-about-their-teens-ai-use/) — chatbot emotional support is the only teen AI use a majority of parents oppose (18% comfortable), and the Character.AI settlement story ran on CBS Mornings AND Glenn Beck, making the concern demonstrably cross-partisan and mass-market. Books at this intersection have one affirmative retail datapoint (*Raising AI*'s list appearances and its [PRH paperback pickup](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262049764/raising-ai/)).

3. **The "Pokémon Go parenting guide" kill (durability).** The fad-decay attack failed on every 2026 dataset — Common Sense's [now-annual census](https://www.commonsensemedia.org/research/a-comprehensive-report-on-teens-tweens-and-ai) still finds roughly three-quarters of teens have used companions, [FAU's peer-reviewed n=3,466 study](https://www.fau.edu/newsdesk/articles/chatbot-ai-study-teens) confirms mass adoption with 13-year-olds most exposed, the companion market grows at [18–31% CAGR](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/ai-companion-market-report), and [Meta is rebuilding teen AI characters](https://techcrunch.com/2026/01/23/meta-pauses-teen-access-to-ai-characters-ahead-of-new-version/), not exiting. This is a capability every major platform ships, not one app with a usage cliff.

4. **The stale-news-cycle kill (durability).** The forward peg calendar is stacked and dateable — the [GUARD Act floor fight](https://rollcall.com/2026/04/30/ban-on-kids-companion-chatbots-advanced-by-senate-committee/), NY ban enforcement, first SB 243 operator safety reports due July 1, 2027, expanding ChatGPT wrongful-death litigation with mass-tort recruitment, annual Common Sense census releases — and each platform parental-dashboard launch manufactures the discovery moments the book monetizes.

5. **The settings-toggle kill (durability).** The claim that platform parental controls would domesticate the problem failed on its own record — identical rails on Instagram did nothing to slow *The Anxious Generation*, litigation documents guardrails failing after safety updates, and rails convert visible use into hidden use, which increases the need for the book's detection chapter.

6. **The legal-evisceration kill (execution).** The skeptic's own strongest form — "a Breakup protocol for suicidal-risk-adjacent situations gets gutted by publisher legal review" — failed when prosecuted: [negligent-publication doctrine](https://corporate.findlaw.com/intellectual-property/publisher-liability-incitement-amp-negligent-publication.html) puts a heavy causation burden on plaintiffs, mainstream publishers routinely ship parent-facing crisis guidance, and more conservative nonprofits ([JED](https://jedfoundation.org/why-your-teen-shouldnt-be-using-ai-companions-and-what-to-do-if-they-are/), APA, [Child Mind](https://childmind.org/article/ai-chatbots-and-teens/)) already publish "what to do if they are" content. Review will hedge the Red Zone chapter toward professional-referral routing, not gut it.

7. **The junk-evidence kill (evidence).** The attack on the arXiv addiction-language study failed — it was accepted at CHI 2026 ([peer-reviewed, ACM DOI](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3772318.3790597)), independently corroborated at [FAccT 2026](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3805689.3812235) and by Drexel follow-up work, and the book's claim as written ("teens describing their own use in withdrawal/tolerance/relapse language") is literally true. The moral-panic-literature attack likewise failed to land, because the skeptics' target is causal-harm and ban claims that the book's calibration-first, anti-ban structure deliberately does not make.

## 6. The Adjudicator's Verdict

The adjudicator's summary judgment, in full:

> The concept survives adversarial review, but only in a revised form — the niche is real and the framing around it is false. Three independent skeptics genuinely tried to find the announced rival and failed: as of July 2026 no traditionally published, book-length, teen-focused, scripts-and-protocols guide to AI-companion attachment exists or is announced, demand is mainstream and cross-partisan (Pew: only 18% of parents are comfortable with teens using chatbots for emotional support), the phenomenon is platformizing rather than fading, and the forward news calendar (GUARD Act floor fight, NY ban enforcement, first SB 243 safety reports due July 1, 2027) stacks pegs into the pub window. But the proposal as written contains claims that are checkably false and would kill it on first editorial read: the shelf is NOT "verifiably empty" (Turkle's *Artificial Intimacy* lands from Little, Brown on Sept 29, 2026; Twenge, Suskind, the AAP, and Susskind populate the adjacent shelf Publishers Weekly has already covered as a wave); JED and the APA have already published the operative core of the Breakup protocol and 72-hour triage in free guides; the headline 72%/33% stats deflate badly under their real definitions and bases (Pew's companionship-specific numbers are 12–16%); the "ban proves migration" spine is a CEO prediction, not data; the settlement half of the platform-combat chapter is legally wrong (confidential, no-admission settlements entitle nonparty parents to nothing); the entire named Track A author bench publicly holds the position opposite the book's "don't ban" hook; and the Labor Day 2026 gate is arithmetically dead as of July 9, 2026. Every one of these is fixable, and the fixes mostly strengthen the book — the honest numbers still sell it, SB 243's real private right of action is better material than the imagined settlement leverage, and "the ban just passed — now what?" is a stronger frame than "you can't ban it." Proceed with the revisions below, a P&L sized to Good Inside/follower-title comps rather than Anxious Generation fantasy, and the author signed before circulation.

All eleven mandated revisions have been applied across this package.

## 7. Go / No-Go Conditions

The proceed decision is conditional, not absolute. Whoever picks this concept up should treat the following as hard decision criteria, in order.

### Gate 1 — The author gate (fatal by design)

**No proposal circulates until a Track A or Track B author is signed.** This condition originated with the tournament's editor judge and was preserved by the adjudicator precisely because a failed search *should* kill the deal: the concept is worth nothing without the clinician. The validated path:

- **Track B is now primary**: a working clinician with an active teen practice paired with a tech-accountability journalist for the reported spine. The structure (named protocols, scripts, triage section, Family AI Agreement) is deliberately load-bearing so a very good author suffices; a celebrity is not required.
- **Track A candidates can only sign under the revised thesis.** Every named bench candidate — Stanford Brainstorm clinicians, Common Sense Media advisors, APA and JED-affiliated experts — publicly holds a restriction-first position (Common Sense/Stanford Brainstorm rated companions an ["unacceptable risk" for under-18s in April 2025](https://www.commonsensemedia.org/press-releases/ai-companions-decoded-common-sense-media-recommends-ai-companion-safety-standards)). The original "DON'T ban your kid's AI girlfriend" hook has therefore been killed; the governing thesis is now **"bans alone backfire — this is the working exit ramp to zero,"** which a Track A clinician can sign without repudiating their institution.
- Damour-class names are endorser/foreword targets, not acquisition targets.
- **Realistic timing:** author signed and proposal out in Q4 2026, with Turkle's September 29, 2026 launch treated as the start of the me-too clock and a crashed spring-2027 pub evaluated against the default 2027–28 window. The original Labor Day 2026 gate is dead and must not appear in any forward plan.

**No-go trigger:** if no author is signed by the end of Q1 2027 — by which point Turkle's launch will have been generating rival proposals for a full quarter — the window economics should be re-run from scratch, with a presumption against proceeding.

### Gate 2 — P&L sizing (proceed only at the honest number)

- Size the advance and P&L to **Good Inside-lane clinician-protocol comps, not *Anxious Generation* comps.** The sober base rate for post-Haidt follower titles is *The Tech Exit*'s Christian-retail-only performance; Haidt's own franchise extension shows he absorbs much of "the Haidt buyer's" repeat demand ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/01/27/nx-s1-5686531/anxious-generation-jonathan-haidt-screen-time-amazing-generation)).
- **Hedge format below $28-hardcover-only economics** (trade paper or simultaneous paperback).
- **Fund the demand proof before circulation:** the Attachment Check-In quiz funnel and the 2,000-parent proprietary survey (including the migration module that converts the book's weakest claim into its ownable original finding) must run pre-proposal, so the package presents measured email-capture and preorder-intent conversion instead of the Haidt analogy.
- Close the Common Sense Media / Child Mind co-brand before circulation — the "exit ramp to zero" reframe makes it signable — and treat Bark/Aura-style monitoring vendors (already collecting $8–32/month in this category) as bundling/affiliate channels.

**No-go trigger:** if the quiz funnel runs and conversion is negligible, the trade-up bet (risk #6) is disproven by the project's own data, and the honest move is to stop.

### Gate 3 — Legal review (budgeted, not optional)

- A legal reviewer is budgeted for the proposal, with two mandates: verify every Chapter 9 claim about [SB 243's private right of action](https://natlawreview.com/article/when-ai-feels-human-californias-sb-243-opens-door-private-lawsuits) and the state copycats, and enforce the settlement caveat (the [Character.AI/Google settlements](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/07/business/character-ai-google-settle-teen-suicide-lawsuit) are confidential and no-admission; they may be cited as narrative pressure only, never as parent entitlement).
- The Red Zone chapter should be drafted expecting publisher legal review to **hedge it toward professional-referral routing** — the validation found review will not gut it (Section 5, scenario 6), but the "no one else can write this" swagger must not return.
- Statistics discipline is a standing legal-adjacent condition: the canonical wordings in Section 3 are mandatory in jacket, marketing, and broadcast prep. Risk #4 (stat deflation on air) is rated high-likelihood *only if unfixed* — the fix is compliance with this table.

### Standing conditions

- Monitor Publishers Marketplace deal flow continuously for the unannounced clinician-fronted rival (risk #9) — speed is the only defense, and a Haidt companion-bot edition is the designated trigger for the crash-pub decision.
- Re-verify the regulatory calendar at each milestone: GUARD Act status ([S.3062](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119/congress/senate-bill/3062/text)), state-ban enforcement, and the July 1, 2027 SB 243 reporting date, which is both a marketing peg and a content deadline.
- Title testing ("Your Kid's AI Best Friend") against the mothers-35-55 demographic before catalog lock; the title remains an untested hypothesis.
