# Book-Concept Package: *Your Kid's AI Best Friend*

**What this is.** A complete, evidence-validated book concept built from open-internet market research in July 2026. A multi-agent research pipeline swept the market, ran a six-concept tournament, put the winner through formal adversarial review, and drafted the acquisition-ready package in this folder. Everything here reflects the *post-adjudication* concept: three original claims were refuted and corrected, and eleven mandatory revisions are applied throughout.

**The concept in one paragraph.** *Your Kid's AI Best Friend: What to Do When the Phone Loves Them Back* is a clinician-authored, scripts-and-protocols parenting guide to teen AI-companion attachment — crisis triage for the first 72 hours, word-for-word conversation scripts, age-tiered Family AI Agreements, a taper-based "Breakup" protocol, and a platform-accountability playbook built on California SB 243. The honest numbers: 72% of US teens have *tried* an AI companion at least once under Common Sense Media's broad definition; Pew's companionship-specific measure finds roughly **1 in 8 teens (~3 million kids)** using a chatbot for emotional support; **1 in 4 teens has, at least once, taken a serious problem to an AI instead of a person**; and only 18% of parents are comfortable with any of it. The claimed niche is deliberately narrow and survived three preemption searches: as of July 2026, no traditionally published, book-length, teen-focused, clinician-authored scripts-and-protocols guide to AI-companion attachment exists or is announced.

## Definition of Done this package was built to meet

1. Topic validated by independent multi-scout convergence, not a single signal.
2. Concept won a scored, multi-persona tournament against five developed alternatives.
3. Survived adversarial adjudication (five skeptics, six fact-checkers), with all 11 mandatory revisions applied.
4. Every load-bearing statistic uses its canonical corrected wording, with an inline citation to a real URL.
5. Competitive frame names every adjacent rival — no "empty shelf" claims.
6. Risks, kill scenarios, research constraints, and provenance disclosed rather than smoothed over.
7. Raw pipeline outputs archived in `research/` so any claim can be traced to source.
