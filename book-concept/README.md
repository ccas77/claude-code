# The Friend Who Never Says No — Book Concept Package

A complete, evidence-backed nonfiction **book concept built from scratch** — starting with nothing but the open internet, then discovering, validating, stress-testing, and packaging a market-ready idea. This folder is designed so that **a stranger can open it, read it in order, and fully evaluate the concept without any outside help.**

> **The concept in one line:** *The Friend Who Never Says No* — a practical field guide to the AI-companion era: how these products were deliberately engineered to out-friend real friends, why a fast-growing slice of kids are already routing their first intimate relationships through them, and a stage-by-stage playbook to keep a 10-to-17-year-old capable of loving a real person.

## How this was produced (methodology)

This package was built by an autonomous multi-agent workflow. No step relied on prior knowledge of the answer; every fact was researched and verified live.

1. **Discovery (6 parallel research scouts).** Each swept one life domain (health, money/work, relationships, tech/AI, meaning/identity, practical self-reliance) for reader pains that are acute, widespread, growing, emotionally charged, *and* underserved by existing books. → 18 sourced candidate pains.
2. **Synthesis.** A publishing strategist ranked the **8 strongest** anchors.
3. **Concept tournament (6 generators + 3 judges).** Six teams built competing concepts from distinct creative angles; a Big Five acquisitions editor, a literary agent, and a market analyst scored all six on **reader pain, urgency, originality, commercial potential, and buildability.**
4. **Executive synthesis.** The two front-runners were fused into a concept that keeps the originality of the highest-ceiling idea while being buildable from public evidence alone (documented in `02`).
5. **Adversarial gauntlet (5 skeptics + 3 verifiers).** Skeptics tried to *kill* the concept on five fronts while analysts re-verified every headline statistic. A publisher-director then issued a **GO-WITH-REVISIONS** decision and corrected the record (documented in `09`).
6. **Build (4 parallel writers).** Produced the dossier, market evidence, proposal, and TOC + sample.
7. **Completeness critic + remediation.** A critic graded the package against the Definition of Done; its punch-list was fixed — missing pieces added (`07`, `08`), factual risks corrected, and the chapter structure reconciled to one canonical outline (documented in `10`).

## Canonical facts (the single source of truth for contested numbers)

To keep every document consistent, these framings are canonical throughout. Full citations live in `04-market-evidence.md`.

- **Teen chatbot usage:** 64% of US teens 13–17 have used AI chatbots; ~28–30% daily (**Pew**, fielded Sep–Oct 2025). This is *general* usage — never labeled "companion" use.
- **The "72%" figure is Common Sense Media, not Pew**, and its "AI companion" definition includes general-purpose ChatGPT/Claude.
- **Intimacy use is a minority (~12–16% of all teens):** 12% use chatbots for emotional support, 16% for casual conversation (Pew). Sold on *anticipatory* anxiety across the whole parent cohort, not on mass harm.
- **Awareness gap:** 37% of parents know their teen uses AI *companions* (Senate testimony, Sep 2025) — distinct from Pew's 51% *chatbot* awareness. Only 18% of parents are comfortable with emotional-support use (Pew, Feb 2026).
- **Litigation is public-record only;** the Jan 2026 Character.AI/Google settlements were **confidential** (records not unsealed).
- **The science is a precautionary hypothesis, not settled fact** — no minor-specific outcome evidence yet; reverse causation conceded.
- **Comp economics: Turkle-scale (75k–250k), not Haidt's "2M+"** (a publisher marketing figure, not audited).
- **Forthcoming direct competitor:** Sherry Turkle, *Artificial Intimacy* (Little, Brown, **Sept 29, 2026**) — unpublished; its contents are described only as *per the publisher's catalog listing*.

## Scope, honesty, and constraints

- **Built entirely from public evidence.** By design the book (and this package) requires **no** private access to grieving families or living minors — a deliberate buildability constraint.
- **No inventing.** Every statistic is cited or explicitly labeled an estimate. Claims that could not be verified were dropped or softened during adversarial review (see the "Claims Corrected or Dropped" section of `09`).
- **This is a concept package, not a manuscript or a market forecast.** The P&L and TAM in `08` are clearly-labeled illustrative models to react to. The most important open variable — the platform author — is called out honestly rather than hand-waved.
- **Nothing here was published, uploaded, or submitted anywhere.** It is a local deliverable only.
