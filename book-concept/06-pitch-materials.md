## 7. Title and Subtitle: The Decision

**Recommended:**

# *Your Kid's AI Best Friend*
### *What to Do When the Phone Loves Them Back — Scripts, House Rules, and the Way Out*

**Why it wins.** It survives the three-second thumbnail test: it names the buyer ("your kid"), the phenomenon ("AI best friend"), and the shelf (parenting) without a wasted word, and "the phone loves them back" does the category-defining work — this is not another screen-time book. The subtitle front-loads the deliverables (scripts, house rules) that separate it from the diagnosis shelf, and "the way out" promises the exit ramp without ever being readable as "learn to live with your kid's AI girlfriend" — the earlier "out-relate the machine" phrasing was retired from the subtitle for exactly that reason. Critically, the title carries no ban-vs.-no-ban position: the marketing frame is "the ban just passed — now what?", and the thesis ("bans alone backfire — this is the working exit ramp to zero") lives in the copy, where it can be stated with the precision it needs.

**Alternatives considered:**

| Title | Case for | Case against |
|---|---|---|
| *The Perfect Listener: Parenting in the Age of Artificial Intimacy* | Elegant; names the mechanism; strong audio/podcast title | Fails the thumbnail test — reads as a communication or self-help book; judges flagged this in tournament. Retained *inside* the book as the named diagnosis ("the perfect-listener problem"). |
| *The Breakup: How to End Your Teen's Relationship With an AI — and Rebuild the Real Ones* | Owns the book's most distinctive protocol; high curiosity | Leads with the crisis minority; reads as prescribing an ending the calibration chapters don't always recommend; risks the moral-panic shelf. |
| *Now What?: The Parent's Playbook for the AI Companion Era* | Rides the post-ban news frame directly | Generic at thumbnail; dates itself to the legislative cycle; cedes the emotional hook. |
| *Out-Relate the Machine* | The thesis as a flag | Too abstract for the duty-purchase and gift mechanics; requires the subtitle to do all the work. |

**Commitment:** the recommended title is a strong paper hypothesis, not a tested fact. Title and subtitle go to a structured test against the core demographic (mothers 35–55 of kids 10–17, recruited from the quiz funnel) before catalog lock, with *The Perfect Listener* and *The Breakup* as the tested alternates. If "AI Best Friend" reads flip rather than urgent to that panel, the decision reverts to the test winner.
