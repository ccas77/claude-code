# Executive Summary

**The deliverable:** a market-ready thriller **series concept** for a solo indie author, with the subgenre chosen by evidence and the concept designed around indie economics. Built from open-internet research via a multi-agent pipeline (parallel research → scored evaluation → adversarial skeptics → concept tournament → judge panel → skeptics → synthesis → completeness review).

---

## 1. The subgenre — chosen by research, then stress-tested

Fifteen thriller subgenres were scored on eight weighted criteria for a solo author with no platform, modest budget, and AI-assisted drafting. Raw winner: **romantic-suspense/thriller crossover (8.7)**. But three independent skeptic agents proved a *true* crossover imposes a fatal **dual-craft tax** (mastering romance *and* thriller, intertwined, every book) and a **discoverability trap** (no native Amazon crossover category; the also-bought engine punishes mis-shelving).

**The resolution — the winning position:**

> ### The "Obsession Thriller"
> A **single-craft psychological/domestic thriller**, thriller-shelved, carrying **dark-romance emotional voltage** (obsession, toxic intimacy, *"I shouldn't want them"*) delivered entirely with thriller tools — **with no guaranteed happy ending.** A real, earned twist is the promise and the line against dark romance.

This captures the #1 *demand* signal (desire + danger, where 2026 reader energy is migrating) at the #1 *buildability* profile (single-craft, no gatekeeper). It exploits genuine **white space** between clinical twist-thrillers (McFadden — plot but no charge) and dark romance (charge but mandatory HEA + romance craft).

**A transparency note on the scoring:** the final position is a deliberate *synthesis*, not simply the top row of the table. Romantic-suspense scored #1 (8.7) on raw demand; single-craft psychological thriller scored #8 (7.7) on raw buildability. The chosen "Obsession Thriller" is the **intersection** the eight-criteria table can't score directly — it takes the *demand* that made romantic-suspense win and delivers it at the *buildability/discoverability* profile of single-craft psych thriller. The adversarial pass is what surfaced that intersection; we followed the *evidence and the stress-test*, not a single column. *Details: `thriller-subset-market-evaluation.md`, `skeptic-and-reconciliation.md`.*

## 2. The concept — *FEED* (Book 1: *FOR YOU*)

A 6-concept tournament (judge panel + skeptics) produced the winner and fixed a twist-logic flaw before finalizing.

**One-sentence pitch:**
> A grief-hollowed data annotator who has memorized every frame of a wellness influencer's life talks her way into becoming the woman's live-in assistant to save her — and swears the feed *chose* her for it, right up until she's standing in the dead last-girl's room and can no longer hide that no one aimed her here: she found the wound and walked in, the same way she did once before.

*You* (Kepnes) × *The Silent Patient*, for the TikTok era, with *The Housemaid*'s bingeability.

- **Protagonist engine:** Wren — the reader's own 2 a.m. scrolling habit given a body; a psychologically-*opaque* (not lying) obsessive you root for against yourself.
- **Antagonist engine:** three human layers — Eloise (who hired her superfan *knowingly*), Dane (a growth-hacker monetizing whales, who buried the last assistant), and Wren's own self-deceiving "rescuer" pattern. **No machine villain**; the algorithm is an *amoral amplifier*, not a conspirator.
- **Twist architecture:** twin twists re-sequenced to *reinforce* — a human crime (Twist One) that clears the field so the true reveal (Twist Two: *no one aimed her; she's done this before*) is **assembled from 14 planted clues**, not retracted. Earned, "of-course" on reread.
- **Series engine:** **FEED — connected standalones**, a new obsessive narrator + platform each book, bound by the off-page amplifier. *"A new obsession every book, same dread."* No recurring couple → author-brand backlist.

*Full concept: `../04-final-concept/`. Sample chapter demonstrates the voice, hooks, and clue-planting.*

## 3. Why it sells, and why it scales
- **Buy Book 1:** the most **phone-native** obsession thriller on the shelf — a universally-felt hook (parasocial 2 a.m. closeness) turned into a knife; a magnetic morally-grey narrator; a twist that rereads the whole book; dark-romance intensity with no genre-bending.
- **Continue the series:** the real horror is a system that *never stops* producing lonely obsessives and bleeding creators — so every book promises another irresistible obsession, same dread, another earned twist. Rapid-release on author brand (the McFadden/Kepnes engine).

## 4. The commercial plan (indie-native)
- **Channel:** **KU-exclusive at launch** (where the reader lives; read-through + page-reads); print for trust; **defer audio + direct to year 2+**, then layer for margin.
- **Cadence:** **stockpile Books 1–3, rapid-release ~monthly** to beat the 30-day cliff; connected standalones for read-through *and* discoverability.
- **Pricing:** $2.99→$4.99 ladder, $9.99 box set; $0.99/free only as funnel pulses.
- **Acquisition:** reader-magnet prequel → newsletter → ARC team — the low-cash system that makes launches land.
- **Packaging:** thriller-only categories, coherent also-bought graph, phone-glow franchise cover system, twist-promising blurb in reader vocabulary.
- **Economics reality:** first year is a **backlist-building investment** (often net-negative); meaningful income arrives with a 5+ book backlist over years 2–4. *Details: folders `05` and `06`.*
