# Decision Log

Every material executive decision, with rationale. Autonomy directive: all calls made without user input.

| # | Decision | Rationale | Alternatives rejected |
|---|----------|-----------|-----------------------|
| D1 | Treat "no publishing" as: no uploading to KDP/retailers/ACX, no distribution — but committing deliverables to the git feature branch IS permitted (version control, not publication). | The guardrail targets publishing the *product*; git is the required workspace per branch instructions. | Not committing at all (would lose work in ephemeral container). |
| D2 | Choose the subgenre by a scored, evidence-based tournament across all 15 named subsets before designing any concept. | Prompt requires the subset be chosen "through research, not personal preference." | Picking a hot subgenre (e.g. domestic thriller) by intuition. |
| D3 | Orchestrate with a multi-agent Workflow: parallel subset research → cross-cutting economics/reader/comps/packaging → synthesis scoring → skeptic refutation → reconciliation. | Prompt explicitly mandates aggressive delegation, parallel threads, tournament logic, and adversarial testing. | Single-threaded manual research (slower, less adversarial rigor). |
| D4 | Weight the scoring model toward revenue potential (×2), buildability for a solo author (×1.5), and series potential (×1.5). | Indie economics live or die on read-through and on whether one person can actually produce the book without gatekeepers. | Equal weighting (would over-reward big-but-saturated or hard-to-build subgenres). |
| D5 | Require every research agent to attach real source URLs per claim and flag estimates. | "No Inventing" guardrail; Definition of Done requires defensible market evidence. | Trusting model priors on market stats. |

| D6 | Reject the raw scoring winner (romantic-suspense crossover) *in its literal form* and adopt the adversarially-refined **"Obsession Thriller"** — single-craft psych/domestic thriller with dark-romance voltage, thriller-shelved, no HEA. | All 3 skeptics independently proved a true crossover imposes a dual-craft tax + discoverability trap fatal to a solo author. The refinement keeps the demand signal (desire+danger) at the best buildability profile. | (a) Literal crossover — fails adversarial test. (b) Retreat to runner-up espionage — skeptics unanimously advised against (narrower, research-heavy, male-skewing). (c) Full dark-romance pivot — abandons thriller craft + adds HEA obligation. |
| D7 | Run a 6-concept tournament (distinct obsession "lenses") with a 3-judge panel + 3 skeptics rather than designing one concept. | Prompt mandates tournament logic and adversarial testing; produced 6 viable concepts (all ≥76/100) and caught a twist-logic flaw a single draft would have shipped. | Designing one concept by intuition. |
| D8 | Select **FEED / *FOR YOU*** (parasocial-digital, 84.7) over near-ties, and **graft** the best element of each runner-up (dead-predecessor-as-presence; "archive answers back" interstitials). | Highest demand/discoverability (universally legible phone-native hook), cleanest single-craft buildability (no forensic/medical gatekeeper), strongest repeatable series engine. | Crime-scene-cleaner (84.0, more research burden); griefbot (84.0, tech-thriller shelving risk). |
| D9 | Apply the skeptics' twist fix: demote the algorithm to **amoral amplifier**, hand the crime to a **human (Dane)**, and make Wren's prior death a **failure-to-save (not a murder)** so Twist Two is an earned self-deception reveal, not a forbidden "lied-then-confessed." | All 3 skeptics flagged the original twin-twist as self-cancelling and Twist Two as the forbidden structure. The fix makes all 14 clues discriminate cleanly toward one earned reading. | Shipping the original twin-twist (would produce "unearned twist" 1-stars — the #1 reader complaint). |
| D10 | **Series as connected standalones on author brand**, not a recurring couple/protagonist. | Resolves the standalone-discovers-vs-series-reads-through tension; matches McFadden/Regan/Ross indie models; lets Books 2–3 reuse the *machine*, not the cast (cheap backlist depth). | Single recurring protagonist (gates new readers) or pure standalones (weak read-through). |
| D11 | **KU-exclusive at launch; defer audio + direct to year 2+.** | Cold-start solo author's binding constraint is discovery; KU is the discovery engine for this exact reader. Audio ($2–5k/title) and direct (needs owned traffic) are poor first-year bets. | Wide-from-launch (forfeits Amazon discovery); audio-at-launch (cash drain before LTV proven). |
| D12 | Recommend **_FOR YOU_** as Book 1 title (over SEEN, WATCH TIME, etc.). | Triple meaning (the "For You" feed / a dedication / "I did this for you"), most platform-legible and BookTok-native. | Others retained as documented alternates. |
| D13 | Author must **own voice, twist logic, and line-level craft**; AI used for scaffolding/ideation only. | A voice-driven psych thriller lives on prose; thin AI prose would break the "magnetic narrator" promise (see risk R8). | Fully AI-drafted prose (quality/authenticity risk). |
