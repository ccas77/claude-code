## D. Factual / logical errors and contradictions found

- **Twist is genuinely earned — confirmed against the concept's own clue list.** Post-fix, Twist One (Dane's human crime + amoral amplifier) and Twist Two (Wren inserted herself; a prior "helped" woman died) no longer cannibalize: Dane's guilt re Mara and Wren's guilt re her past are independent, so the 14 clues discriminate cleanly toward "she did this before and it ended in a death." Clues 1–7 and 14 concretely support that reading. **No logic hole.**
- **One honest nuance on the twist, not an error:** the *"no one aimed her / the feed didn't choose me"* half of Twist Two is not a concealed fact — the narration openly shows Wren doing the finding (geolocating, cataloguing) from page one. So that half is an *emotional/interpretive* reveal (dropping her self-delusion), not an information reveal. The docs are explicit about this ("the lie she tells herself, not the reader"), so it is intentional and fine — but marketing should sell "a twist that rereads the whole book" (true) rather than implying a hidden-fact plot twist, or savvy readers may cry foul.
- **"She's done this before" micro-copy risks the exact mis-read the fix removed.** The blurb one-liner *"She came to save her. She's done this before."* and the pitch's *"the same way she did once before"* can read as *serial killer* — the very "repeat predator" framing the tournament deliberately cut. Internally the docs are consistent (she's a repeat *rescuer*, prior death = failure-to-save, not murder), but the marketing phrasing is ambiguous. Keep it, but ensure surrounding copy steers "again" toward *pattern of engulfing love*, not *body count*.
- **No cross-document contradiction in the concept itself.** Midpoint ("Eloise hired her knowingly") and Twist Two ("no one aimed her") are compatible: the *algorithm/feed* didn't aim her (Twist Two), while *Eloise* separately chose her superfan from the applicant pool (midpoint) — different agents. Coherent, though see fix #3 for the sample-chapter execution risk.
- **Estimates are flagged throughout** (`[estimate]`/`[est.]` in economics, pricing, monetization, comps, reader-language) and the "nothing was published/uploaded" guardrail is stated in README and decision-log. No fabricated statistics detected; research JSON and markdown carry real source URLs.

**Bottom line:** the package is complete and internally sound. Fix the four items above — file this review (done), reconcile the numeric figures, resolve the sample-chapter timeline, and add one sentence owning the synthesized-lane choice — and it is fully airtight.
