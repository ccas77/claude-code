# Commercial Positioning & Pitch

Everything a marketer needs on one page: the position, the pitch, the hook, the titles.

---

## Commercial positioning statement

> **FEED** is a series of single-craft psychological thrillers — thriller-shelved (Amazon **Psychological Thrillers** primary, **Domestic Thriller** secondary) — that deliver dark-romance emotional voltage (obsession, toxic intimacy, *"I shouldn't want to be this close"*) **entirely through thriller tools, with no guaranteed happy ending.** A real, earned twist is the promise and the line that separates it from dark romance. Comps: *You* (Kepnes), *Verity* (Hoover), *Gone Girl*, *The Silent Patient*, *The Housemaid* (McFadden bingeability). ~85,000 words, 60–70 short chapters each ending on a *Wren-knows-too-much* or *wrongness-in-the-house* hook, broken by **screen-content interstitials** (captions, DMs, comment threads, annotation logs) for pace and screenshot-native BookTok shareability. **Standalone-complete, series-scalable on author brand** (connected standalones, no recurring couple). Solo-indie buildable: contemporary phone-native setting, one primary location plus screens, no procedural/forensic/medical/legal gatekeeper burden, single close-first-person POV for fast drafting, rapid-release native.

## One-sentence pitch

> A grief-hollowed data annotator who has memorized every frame of a wellness influencer's life talks her way into becoming the woman's live-in assistant to save her — and swears the feed *chose* her for it, right up until she's standing in the dead last-girl's room and can no longer hide that no one aimed her here: she found the wound and walked in, the same way she did once before.

**Elevator version:** *You* meets *The Silent Patient* for the TikTok era — the follower who knew too much moves in.

---

## The hook (BookTok / ad copy)

> *"She knew everything about the influencer she loved — the gate code, the bruise the ring light hid, the tea at 3 a.m. She talked her way into the house to save her. She keeps telling you the feed reached for her, chose her, put this woman in front of her.
> The last girl who slept in that room believed it too.
> She doesn't."*

**Why it works:** universally legible (everyone has a parasocial 2 a.m. feeling), repeatable in one breath, ends on a dread-drop, and seeds the twist without spoiling it. Screenshot-native.

## Title strategy

**Series name:** **FEED** — one syllable, ownable, on-theme (the platform feed + feeding an obsession + being fed to it), scales across books, looks bold at thumbnail.

**Book 1 title — recommendation: _FOR YOU_.**

| Candidate | Read | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| **FOR YOU** ⭐ | The algorithm's "For You" page *and* a love-letter dedication *and* the predatory "I did this for you." Triple meaning; instantly platform-legible. | **Primary pick** — most resonant, most BookTok-native. |
| SEEN | The read-receipt "Seen," and the book's emotional core ("finally being seen was the trap"). | Strong alt; slightly generic in search. |
| WATCH TIME | Platform metric + surveillance. | Good, cooler, less emotional. |
| THE FOLLOWER | Literal + ominous. | Clear but flatter. |
| SAVED POSTS | "Saved" (bookmarked) + "saved" (rescued) — the rescuer lie. | Clever; weaker at thumbnail. |
| THE ALGORITHM KNOWS | On-nose; risks tech-thriller mis-shelf. | Avoid as title (fine as tagline). |

**Subtitle (keyword-loaded genre convention):** *An absolutely gripping psychological thriller with a twist you won't see coming* — indexed for search, sets the promise. (Rotate exact adjective stack per packaging.md §6.)

**Series data (KDP series field, not subtitle):** *(FEED, Book 1)*.

## Positioning guardrails (do not break)

1. **Shelve in Thriller, never Romance.** Single primary category coherence is the whole discoverability thesis.
2. **No guaranteed HEA.** The earned dark ending is the differentiator from dark romance — market it honestly as "no safe endings."
3. **One craft.** The obsession is *voltage delivered by thriller tools*, never a co-equal romance arc.
4. **The twist is assembled, never retracted.** Market "a twist that rereads the whole book," which the concept can actually deliver (see the 14 planted clues).
