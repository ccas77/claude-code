# Series Bible & Book One Outline — *FEED*

A production document: the series rules, the cast, and a chapter-level outline of Book 1 (*FOR YOU*) a ghostwriter or the author could draft from directly.

---

# PART A — SERIES BIBLE

## Series concept
**FEED** — a set of **connected standalone** psychological thrillers. Each book: a different **obsessive first-person narrator** whose parasocial fixation on a public online figure collapses into the real world and ends in an earned, dark twist. **No recurring protagonist or couple.** The spine is the **platform and its recommendation system as an amoral, off-page force.**

## The five iron rules (per book)
1. **One obsessive narrator, close first person, psychologically opaque** (withholds from *herself*, never cheats the reader).
2. **One public online figure** as the object of obsession (influencer / podcaster / musician / dead creator's account / AI companion).
3. **One primary domestic location** the narrator infiltrates + screens.
4. **A human crime with a mundane motive** in Act 2; the platform is *amplifier*, never villain.
5. **The true twist is the narrator's self-deception, assembled from planted clues** — dark ending, no guaranteed HEA.

## Series arc (the off-page amplifier)
The recommendation engine never changes its nature and is never defeated — it simply keeps rewarding public vulnerability and concentrating "whales." Each book, it produces a new lonely obsessive and a new bleeding creator. **Recurring texture (not plot gates):** Dane's growth agency reappears in the background; the moderation-annotation gig underworld recurs; a journalist slowly assembling the pattern across books can become a light meta-thread for long-time readers.

## Tone & style guide
- **Voice:** intimate, controlled, blackly precise; competence as seduction; tenderness that reads as surveillance.
- **Structure:** 60–70 chapters, **1,000–2,000 words each**, every chapter ends on a hook (*knows-too-much* or *wrongness-in-the-house*).
- **Interstitials:** captions, DM threads, comment sections, annotation logs, engagement graphs — 1/2–1 page, for pace and screenshot-native shareability. ~1 per 2–3 chapters.
- **Length:** ~85,000 words. **No explicit HEA. Earned dark resolution.**
- **Research burden:** low — annotation work, engagement metrics, basic OSINT (reflection geolocation, posting-cadence analysis). *Googleable in a weekend.*

# PART B — BOOK ONE OUTLINE (*FOR YOU*)

## Principal cast
- **Wren D.**, 29 — narrator; content-moderation data annotator; superfan of @honestlyeloise; carries a buried prior "help" that ended in a death.
- **Eloise Vane / @honestlyeloise**, 34 — wellness influencer, 1.2M followers; warmer, sadder, more dangerous in person; hired Wren *knowing*.
- **Dane Coles**, 41 — Eloise's growth-hacking manager; runs the whale-mining and the live-in "assistant" pipeline; killed Mara to bury the scheme.
- **Mara Osei** (dead before page one) — the previous assistant; her archived account and auto-forwarding inbox haunt the house.
- **Camille** — a moderator-annotation coworker / the one real friend Wren keeps at arm's length (outside eyes; a moral mirror).
- **The name Wren can't say** — the woman from before (revealed obliquely); the engine of Twist Two.

## Four-act structure (with the twist scaffolding)

### ACT 1 — SCREEN (≈ ch. 1–15, 0–22%)
- **Open on the annotation floor** (see sample chapter): Wren labeling human suffering; establish her competence, numbness, and the comfort of @honestlyeloise. Plant: *"the money from before,"* the passport hesitation, the fluency.
- Eloise's 3 a.m. "hiring someone" post. Wren already knows the gate code pattern, the tea, the neighborhood.
- Wren applies as **"Wren D."** Interview. Near-miss of recognition. **She gets the job.**
- **Act 1 hook:** she steps into the real house and it smells of eucalyptus — a sensory memory she refuses to finish.

### ACT 2A — HOUSE (≈ ch. 16–32, 22–45%)
- Living inside the real Eloise; intimacy deepens; the reader falls for it too.
- Wrongnesses accumulate: **Mara's repainted room**, the locked studio, the wine bottles migrating recycling→trash, Dane's presence, the **"high-intent" dashboard column**, the **Kettle Club** ledger, the **second burner phone.**
- Wren repaints the **handprint-height smudge** without being asked.
- **Interstitials** seed Mara's still-auto-forwarding inbox and one **scheduled post queued to publish.**
- **Act 2A hook:** the archived missing-person post for Mara that Eloise pinned then deleted.

### ACT 2B — COLLAPSE OF DISTANCE (≈ ch. 33–40, 45–55%)
- The relationship turns genuinely intimate *and* predatory. Desire and dread fuse.
- Wren assembles that **Mara didn't move — she died.**
- **TWIST ONE lands (≈ 50%):** Dane's exploitation scheme; the algorithm as amoral amplifier; Dane staged Mara's "departure." *Mundane motive: money, cover-up.*

### MIDPOINT — THE DOMESTIC FLIP (≈ ch. 41, 55%)
- **Eloise reveals she has a folder on Wren and hired her knowingly** — "you always did like the blue mug." The watched chose the watcher. The ground under every "the feed chose me" liquefies — but the reader still reads it as *Eloise's* manipulation, not yet Wren's self-deception.

### ACT 3 — REAL WORLD → BACK INTO THE HOUSE (≈ ch. 42–66, 55–95%)
- Eloise's next **"big vulnerable drop"** is spun up for engagement; the clock starts.
- Dane moves to contain **the second inconvenient girl** (Wren). Genuine survival danger.
- Wren's "help" for Eloise escalates into **the same smothering control** her buried past predicts — the reader starts to feel it before Wren admits it.
- **TWIST TWO lands (≈ 85–90%):** the self-deception breaks. *No one aimed her. She found the wound and walked in — both times.* The eucalyptus/bathwater memory completes; the name; the settlement; the order. *"I came to save her" is the mask her guilt wears.*
- **Climax:** the two women alone in the house during the live drop; Dane bearing down. Wren must choose whether to become — one last time — the engulfing weapon of her own history, or to break the pattern at real cost.

### RESOLUTION (≈ ch. 67–70, 95–100%)
- **Fully resolves** Wren, Eloise, Dane — dark, earned, no clean HEA. (Recommended: Wren breaks the pattern by *letting go* — the one act her history proves she can't do — at a devastating price; Dane exposed or dead; Eloise survives but changed, or the cost falls where it hurts most. Final image returns to a screen: the amplifier, untouched, already surfacing the next wound to the next lonely watcher.)
- **The amplifier survives** → series engine intact.
- **Final line** rereads the title *FOR YOU.*

## Read-through / cliffhanger design
Each chapter closes on one of: *(a) a thing Wren knows she shouldn't,* *(b) a wrongness in the house,* *(c) a self-exculpating line that the alert reader clocks as a tell.* The book ends **resolved** (standalone integrity) but the **last interstitial** — the feed surfacing a new creator's 3 a.m. post to a new lonely account — is the pull into Book 2.

## Buildability checklist (why a solo author ships this fast)
- ✅ One POV, one primary location, contemporary setting.
- ✅ No forensic/medical/legal procedure to research.
- ✅ Texture is googleable in a weekend (annotation, engagement metrics, OSINT basics).
- ✅ Short chapters + interstitials → drafts fast, rapid-release friendly.
- ✅ Series bible above means Books 2–3 reuse the *machine*, not the characters — the hard part (the engine) is built once.
