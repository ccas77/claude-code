# Pricing & Format Strategy

Grounded in the indie economics research (`../research-data/economics.md`) and packaging conventions (`../research-data/packaging.md`). All royalty mechanics are current KDP rules; income figures flagged **[estimate]** are community/industry aggregates, not Amazon-published.

---

## 1. The governing principle

**The unit of profit is the series, not the book.** Every pricing decision below serves one goal: **maximize series read-through and KU page-reads over lifetime**, not per-title margin. Acquisition is paid once at Book 1; revenue compounds across the series.

---

## 2. Ebook pricing ladder (the core plan)

| Title | Price | Royalty band | Net/sale [est.] | Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Book 1** | **$2.99** (launch) → test **$3.99** | 70% | ~$2.05 → ~$2.75 | Entry drug. Kept cheap to lower the cost of the first hit. Occasional **$0.99 / free** promo pulses as a funnel. |
| **Books 2–3** | **$3.99** | 70% | ~$2.75 | Standard mid-series price once read-through is proven. |
| **Books 4+** | **$4.99** | 70% | ~$3.45 | Committed readers are price-insensitive by Book 4 (80–90% read-through). |
| **Box set (Books 1–3)** | **$9.99** | 70% | ~$6.85 | Whole-series impulse buy; also a "start here" for new readers. Priced below buying separately. |

**Hard rules from the research:**
- **Never price a full novel at $2.98 or below** except as a deliberate promo — it's the line between 35% and 70% royalty. *(economics.md §2)*
- **$0.99 and free are funnel tools, not earners** ($0.35/sale). Use Book 1 free/discounted *pulses* around launches and BookBub-style promos, not permanently, unless read-through data justifies permafree later. *(economics.md §3)*
- **Charm pricing** (`.99` endings) is standard. *(packaging.md §7)*

---

## 3. Kindle Unlimited: the primary channel — enroll, exclusively, at least to start

**Decision: enroll all books in KDP Select / KU.** *(logged in decision-log)*

Rationale from the research:
- Thriller/psych is **~73% KU-penetrated**; the target reader is a **heavy KU subscriber**. *(economics.md §1)*
- **KU borrowers read through series at higher rates than buyers** — the next book is free to them. For an obsession-thriller series engineered for bingeing, this is the whole game. *(economics.md §3)*
- **KENP ~$0.0043/page**; an ~85,000-word book normalizes to **~400 KENP**, paying **~$1.70 on a full read** [estimate] — *on top of* any sales, and it stacks across the series with near-frictionless read-through. *(economics.md §1; 100k ≈ 400–500 KENP)*

**The cost:** KU requires **Amazon exclusivity** (no Apple/Kobo/Google/wide for enrolled titles). We accept this **for the launch phase and likely the life of the series**, because a no-platform solo author gets *discovery* from Amazon that wide can't replicate cold. Revisit only if/when a direct audience exists (see §5).

---

## 4. Print (paperback POD) — secondary, launch-adjacent

- Publish **KDP Print paperback** at **$14.99** (nets ~$4–5/copy after the ~$5 print cost on a ~350-page book). *(economics.md §4)*
- **Role:** legitimacy, gift/signed copies, "also available in paperback" trust signal, and **price-anchoring** (makes the $3.99 ebook look like the value option). Not a primary revenue stream.
- **Skip Expanded Distribution** initially (worse margin); revisit for backlist.

---

## 5. Audiobook — deferred, then aggressive once the series is proven

- **Do NOT produce audio for the launch.** Production is **$2,000–$5,000+ per title** or a **royalty-share** that halves royalties and locks exclusivity — a poor first-year bet. *(economics.md §5)*
- **Trigger to produce:** once Books 1–3 show strong ebook/KU read-through (proving lifetime value), produce audio starting with Book 1.
- **Model:** ACX (new **50% exclusive / 30% non-exclusive** pooled model) *or* **Voices by INaudio** (wide, ~20% cut) + **Spotify for Authors**. Obsession thrillers with a strong first-person voice are **excellent audio candidates** — the intimate narrator *is* the product. *(economics.md §5)*

---

## 6. Direct sales — the year-2+ margin play

- **Not a cold-start channel** — direct requires you to drive your own traffic. *(economics.md §6)*
- **Sequence:** keep new releases on Amazon/KU for the launch algorithm; once a newsletter list exists, sell **box sets, special editions, and backlist direct** via **Payhip** (free → $29/mo Pro at ~$580/mo sales) with **BookFunnel** delivery, keeping **~95%** of the sale. *(economics.md §6)*
- This is where per-reader margin eventually multiplies — but only *after* the KU-fed audience exists.

---

## 7. Format-by-phase summary

| Phase | Ebook | KU | Print | Audio | Direct |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Launch (Bk 1–3)** | ✅ $2.99–$4.99 | ✅ exclusive | ✅ $14.99 | ❌ | ❌ |
| **Build (Bk 4–6 + box set)** | ✅ ladder | ✅ | ✅ | ▶️ start Bk 1 | ▶️ box sets/backlist |
| **Scale (backlist mature)** | ✅ | ✅ (or test wide) | ✅ +ED | ✅ full series | ✅ storefront |

**Bottom line:** Launch **cheap, exclusive, and ebook/KU-first** to win discovery and read-through; add **print for trust**; defer **audio and direct** until the series proves its lifetime value, then layer them for margin.
