# Metadata, Categories & Keywords — *FEED* Book 1 (*FOR YOU*)

Concrete KDP metadata plan, grounded in `../research-data/packaging.md` §3–6. **Verify all category paths and run keyword competition checks in Publisher Rocket at upload time** — the category tree and competition shift; the *logic* below is stable.

---

## 1. Title / subtitle / series fields

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Title** | FOR YOU |
| **Subtitle** | An absolutely gripping psychological thriller with a twist you won't see coming |
| **Series** | FEED (Book 1) |
| **Author** | *[Pen name — see note]* |
| **Edition language** | English |

**Subtitle logic:** the subtitle is an *indexed keyword surface* (packaging.md §6). It carries the genre descriptor ("psychological thriller") for search relevance *and* the twist promise for reader expectation. Don't repeat title words; don't use "A Novel" alone. Rotate the exact adjective stack ("gripping/addictive/unputdownable") against comps.

**Pen-name note:** brand the *author name*, not one book (the McFadden/Jewell lesson — the name eventually gets bigger than the title). Pick a clean, memorable, search-distinct pen name and keep the whole FEED series under it.

---

## 2. The 3 selectable browse categories (post-2023 KDP system)

Since mid-2023, KDP allows **3 selectable categories**, extended by keyword-triggered nodes (packaging.md §3.1). Mix one credible-traffic shelf with two winnable niches:

1. **Kindle eBooks › Mystery, Thriller & Suspense › Thriller & Suspense › Psychological** *(primary — the coherent home; moderate competition, high intent)*
2. **Kindle eBooks › Mystery, Thriller & Suspense › Thriller & Suspense › Domestic** *(secondary — "grip lit" readers; high intent)*
3. **A narrower, winnable niche** — candidates to check in Publisher Rocket by "Sales to #10": *Crime Fiction › Kidnapping*, *Thriller › Psychological › Serial Killers*-adjacent, or *Suspense* sub-nodes. **Pick the one where a realistic launch day earns a #1 badge.**

> **Rule:** one shelf for credibility/traffic + 1–2 easy niches for the "#1 Best Seller" badge (packaging.md §5). Never spend all three on saturated top shelves an unknown can't rank in.

---

## 3. The 7 backend keyword slots (each ≤50 chars, phrases not words)

**Do NOT** repeat title/subtitle/series words (Amazon already indexes those). Split slots between **search phrases** (demand + low competition) and **category-trigger phrases** (packaging.md §4). Draft set — validate each in Publisher Rocket (target ~1,000–20,000 results + low Competitive Score):

| # | Keyword phrase | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `obsession thriller unreliable narrator` | Core positioning search |
| 2 | `books like You Caroline Kepnes` | Comp-title intent (avoid *author name as brand* trap — phrase as read-alike) |
| 3 | `stalker suspense books for women` | Audience + trope search |
| 4 | `social media influencer thriller` | Fresh, low-competition, on-concept |
| 5 | `psychological suspense twist ending` | High-intent genre search |
| 6 | `domestic thriller booktok` | Discovery-tag trigger |
| 7 | `parasocial obsession novel slow burn` | Long-tail, distinctive, low competition |

*Method (packaging.md §4.2): mine Amazon auto-suggest, reverse-ASIN the comps (You, The Silent Patient, The Housemaid, Verity), and check competitive scores. Refresh keywords quarterly.*

**Prohibited:** "best," "free," "on sale," "new," other authors' names as pure trademarks, and subjective claims. Phrase comps as "books like X," not bare author names.

---

## 4. Book description (A+ / HTML) structure

Follows the 3–4 part blurb formula (packaging.md §2). Full copy in `blurb-back-cover-copy.md`. On the product page:
- **Bold hook line** first (renders above the fold).
- Short punchy paragraphs (mobile-read).
- Close on the unresolved-question tease.
- Add an editorial "**For fans of _You_, _The Silent Patient_, and _The Housemaid_**" line — comp shorthand does trust work (reader-language bank §5a).

---

## 5. Also-bought coherence (the discoverability thesis)

Everything above serves **single-tribe coherence**: this book must land in the *psychological/domestic thriller* also-bought graph, next to McFadden, Kepnes, Michaelides, Jewell — **never** in Romance. That coherence is precisely what the market-evaluation skeptics proved a true crossover forfeits. Metadata discipline is how the concept's core advantage is realized at the retail layer.
