# Cover Direction Brief — *FEED* Book 1 (*FOR YOU*)

For a cover designer (or an AI-image + typography workflow). Grounded in the thriller cover conventions in `../research-data/packaging.md` §1. **Goal: reads as psychological/domestic thriller at thumbnail, signals "obsession + screens," and works as a scalable series template.**

---

## 1. Non-negotiable genre grammar (so it shelves correctly)
From the top-100 thriller cover analysis (packaging.md §1.1):
- **Type leads over image** (~79% of top thriller covers). The **title is the biggest element**, legible "across a parking lot."
- **Condensed sans-serif**, title filling **30–50%** of the cover (Bebas Neue / Trade Gothic / similar).
- **Dark base + one surgical accent.** Start from black/charcoal/navy; add a *single* alarming color.
- **Asymmetric composition, negative space, single-source unnatural light.**
- **Imagery by implication**, not literal scene.

> This must read **Psychological Thriller**, adjacent to McFadden/Kepnes/Michaelides — **never Romance.** Discoverability depends on it.

---

## 2. The concept-specific idea: *phone-glow obsession*

The ownable visual hook for FEED is **the light of a screen on a face in the dark** — intimacy and surveillance in one image.

**Primary cover direction (recommended):**
- **A woman's face, half-lit from below by cold phone/screen glow**, the other half swallowed by black. Eyes down-lit, expression unreadable — *tender or predatory, you can't tell.* Face pushed off-center, lots of black negative space for the title.
- Or: **a single eye reflected in a phone screen**, the reflection slightly *wrong* (not quite matching) — the watcher/watched motif.
- Or (most restrained): **the blue mug on a windowsill at blown-out dawn**, exactly as in the hiring post — with the title huge over it. Ties cover to the book's signature object; very domestic-thriller.

**Accent color:** a single **cold notification-blue** or **acid alert-red** — the color of a screen at 3 a.m. Blue is fresher and more ownable for this concept; red is the genre-safe default.

**Type treatment:** **FOR YOU** in tall condensed caps, possibly with a subtle **screen-glow / backlit** effect or a hairline "seen"/read-receipt cue. Series word **FEED** small and consistent at top or bottom (the series band). Author name condensed; sized to *grow across the series* as the brand builds.

---

## 3. Series template (scalability is the point)

Design **FOR YOU** as **Book 1 of a repeatable system**, because connected standalones live or die on instant "next-in-series" recognition (comps.md — Regan/Pierce/Ross template lesson):
- **Fixed elements every book:** the FEED wordmark position, the condensed title font, the dark base, the phone-glow lighting motif, the accent-color logic, author-name placement.
- **Variable element:** the central image swaps per book's platform/obsession (face-in-phone-glow → podcast mic lit by a screen → a music waveform as a face → a chat bubble glowing in the dark). Same treatment, new object.
- Result: a shelf of FEED books that are unmistakably a set at thumbnail — the author-brand engine the series strategy needs.

---

## 4. Production notes (solo-indie budget)
- **Budget:** ~$150–$800 per cover for series branding (economics.md §9). A strong template makes Books 2+ cheaper (swap the central image).
- **Practical build:** stock or AI-generated central image (face in phone glow) → heavy custom typography → dark grade + single accent. Typography is the hero; spend effort there.
- **Deliverables:** ebook (1600×2560), paperback wrap (with $14.99 spine/back per pricing strategy), and a **square/vertical crop** for BookTok/ads where the phone-glow face reads instantly.
- **A/B test** two directions (face-in-glow vs. blue-mug) on a thumbnail before launch if feasible.

---

## 5. What to avoid
- ❌ Romance cues: couples, warm/saturated palettes, clinch poses, script fonts, metallics — these mis-shelve the book and break the also-bought thesis.
- ❌ Literal "hacker/tech-thriller" imagery (green code, circuit boards) — this is domestic/psychological, not technothriller.
- ❌ Busy scenes. One image, one accent, huge type.
- ❌ A cover that can't be told apart from Book 2 at thumbnail — or one so template-locked it looks generic. Balance recognizable + distinct.
