# Release Cadence & Backlist Strategy

How to schedule production and releases to beat the Amazon visibility cliff, compound read-through, and build a backlist that pays. Grounded in `../research-data/economics.md` §7.

---

## 1. The mechanic we're exploiting: the 30-day cliff + rapid release

Amazon front-loads visibility for new titles: strongest **New Releases** push in the **first 30 days**, stepping down at **60** and **90 days**. Amazon also **penalizes spike-then-silence** (a big launch followed by dead weeks reads as low ongoing demand). *(economics.md §7)*

**The response — rapid release:** publish series books **~30 days apart, no more than ~90 apart**, so each launch (1) re-triggers the 30-day window, (2) sends read-through traffic back through the whole series via also-boughts, and (3) keeps the series algorithmically "sticky." This is why indie thriller authors **write fast, to a formula, and stockpile finished books before launching Book 1.**

> **2024–2026 caveat:** multiple sources (The Bookseller, ALLi) report Amazon algorithm changes causing "volatility in visibility." Rapid release still holds directionally, but expect less predictability than the 2017–2020 heyday. Plan for consistency, not a guaranteed spike.

---

## 2. The launch architecture: stockpile, then rapid-release

**Do not publish Book 1 alone and hope.** The plan:

### Phase 0 — Write ahead (pre-launch, ~6–10 months)
- Draft **Books 1–3 complete** before launching anything. The Obsession Thriller's single-craft, ~85,000-word, short-chapter format is explicitly chosen to make this feasible for a solo author with AI-assisted drafting.
- Build the newsletter/reader-magnet funnel in parallel (see `reader-magnet-newsletter-arc-launch-plan.md`).

### Phase 1 — Rapid-release the opening trilogy (months 0–3)
- **Book 1** launches (with ARC reviews primed).
- **Book 2** at ~day 30, **Book 3** at ~day 60–90.
- Each release re-triggers visibility and pulls readers back to Book 1. Read-through compounds fastest when the next book already exists.

### Phase 2 — Sustain (months 3–18)
- **Books 4–6** every ~60–90 days.
- Drop the **Box Set (1–3)** after Book 4–5 to convert browsers and re-rank the series.
- Begin **audio** on Book 1 once ebook read-through is proven (see pricing strategy §5).

### Phase 3 — Backlist compounding (year 2+)
- The community rule: **indie authors "break out between Book 3 and Book 5"** — enough backlist for read-through and also-boughts to compound. *(economics.md §3)*
- Backlist scaling benchmark: authors with **25+ books** reach a **median ~$3,000/month** [estimate]. The obsession-thriller *connected-standalone* engine (see the series bible) is built to run long without a co-writer.

## 3. Series structure that maximizes read-through *and* discoverability

The concept uses **connected standalones under a strong author brand** (the McFadden/Regan lesson), not one recurring couple:
- **Each book stands alone** (fresh obsessive narrator / fresh case) → aids discovery, lowers the "must start at Book 1" barrier, and lets any book be the entry point.
- **A shared spine** (recurring world / recurring investigator or motif / escalating meta-mystery) → gives series read-through and a reason to continue.
- **Author name becomes the brand** — once read-through exists, the author name grows larger than the title on covers (the Jewell/Ross pattern). Design the cover template to scale toward that (see cover brief).

This resolves the classic tension: standalones discover well but read through poorly; tight series read through well but gate new readers. Connected standalones get **both**.

---

## 4. Cadence summary

| Window | Action | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-launch | Finish Books 1–3; build funnel | Never launch Book 1 alone |
| Day 0 | Book 1 + ARC reviews | Trip the 30-day boost |
| Day ~30 | Book 2 | Re-trigger visibility, read-through |
| Day ~60–90 | Book 3 | Compound; series "sticky" |
| Q2–Q3 | Books 4–6 + Box Set | Cross book 3–5 breakout line |
| Year 2+ | Backlist + audio + direct box sets | Compound lifetime value |

**Bottom line:** stockpile three books, rapid-release ~monthly to beat the cliff, structure as connected standalones for read-through *and* discoverability, and let the backlist compound past Book 5 where indie careers actually break.
