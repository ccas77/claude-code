# Reader Magnet, Newsletter, ARC & Launch Plan

The low-cost customer-acquisition system that makes a rapid-release launch land. This is not direct revenue — it is the machine whose payoff is measured in **series lifetime value**. Grounded in `../research-data/economics.md` §8.

---

## 1. The system in one diagram

```
Reader Magnet (free prequel novella)
        │  exchanged for email via BookFunnel
        ▼
   Newsletter list  ──►  ARC team (subset)  ──►  launch-day reviews
        │                                              │
        ▼                                              ▼
  warm day-one sales/borrows  ──────────────►  trips the 30-day Amazon boost
        │                                              │
        ▼                                              ▼
     read-through across the paid series  ◄──  also-boughts / halo
```

Newsletter + magnet + ARC = the acquisition system; the series is the monetization. *(economics.md §8)*

---

## 2. Reader magnet — the funnel's front door

- **A specific, desirable free asset converts far better than "sign up for my newsletter."** *(economics.md §8)*
- **Build a prequel novella / "Book 0"** in the series world — an obsession-thriller short featuring a *different* obsessive narrator (connected-standalone engine makes this trivial). It doubles as a **voice sample** and a **story-world teaser**.
- **Delivered via BookFunnel** (~$20+/yr) — solves "how do I get this file onto my Kindle."
- Title the magnet with the same dark-hook grammar as the series so it *sells the brand*, not just the freebie.

---

## 3. Newsletter — the highest-ROI channel indies have

- Newsletter subscribers **convert to buyers at meaningfully higher rates than social followers.** Email is the highest-ROI channel. *(economics.md §8)*
- **Platform:** start free/low-cost (MailerLite tier), automate a **welcome sequence** that delivers the magnet, sets the "dark, twisty, no-safe-endings" brand promise, and primes for Book 1.
- **List-building tactics (cheap, from the research):**
  - **Group promos** (BookFunnel/StoryOrigin): 10–200+ authors pool free/discounted books on one landing page, each promotes to their list — one of the cheapest list-building tactics available.
  - **Newsletter swaps:** cross-promote with other psych/domestic-thriller authors to their lists — reach without ad spend.
- **Cadence:** value-forward (recommendations, behind-the-obsession notes, cover reveals) between launches; hard-sell only at releases.

---

## 4. ARC team — priming the launch algorithm

- An **ARC team** gets the book early in exchange for an **honest launch-day review**. *(economics.md §8)*
- **Debut-realistic size:** ~**20–50 readers**; **review conversion ~20–40%** → recruit ~50, expect **~10–20 reviews** in the first two weeks.
- **Why it matters:** early reviews = social proof + algorithm signal on day one → helps trip the 30-day boost → drives read-through. Recruit the team *from* the newsletter.
- Use **BookFunnel** for ARC distribution (same tool as the magnet).

---

## 5. Preorder decision

- **Preorder is optional and double-edged:** it lets fans buy early but *spreads* sales across days, weakening the day-one rank spike that the algorithm rewards. **Recommendation:** for Book 1 (no fanbase yet), **skip preorder** and concentrate sales on launch day. For Books 2+ (warm list), **use a short 1–2 week preorder** to capture the eager readers while still landing a strong release day.

---

## 6. Launch-week playbook (Book 1)

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| **T-30 to T-7** | ARC team reading; magnet funnel live; newsletter warming; cover/blurb finalized; categories/keywords set |
| **T-3** | Newsletter "3 days" tease; remind ARC team of launch-day review window |
| **Day 0** | Launch at **$2.99**; newsletter blast; ARC reviews post; BookTok hook videos go live |
| **Day 1–3** | Concentrate all traffic to spike rank → New Releases boost; monitor also-boughts |
| **Day 7–14** | First promo pulse if needed; collect reviews to ~15–25; confirm read-through to Book 2 |
| **Day ~30** | **Book 2 launches** → re-trigger visibility, pull readers back to Book 1 |

---

## 7. Budget reality (first-year, from the research) [estimates]

- Covers ~$150–$800 each (series branding); editing ~$800–$2,500/book; ads highly variable ($500–$5,000+/yr); BookFunnel + email tool ~$20–$100/yr.
- **First-year income is usually small and often net-negative** — this is a **backlist-building investment phase**. Meaningful money arrives with a 5+ book backlist over years 2–4. *(economics.md §9)*
- The funnel above is deliberately the **cheapest high-leverage** part of the system: magnet + list + ARC + swaps + group promos require time, not cash — the right allocation for a modest-budget solo author.
