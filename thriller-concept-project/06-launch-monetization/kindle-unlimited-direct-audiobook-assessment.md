# Kindle Unlimited / Direct-Sales / Audiobook Assessment

A channel-by-channel verdict for *FEED*, grounded in `../research-data/economics.md`. Bottom line up front: **KU-exclusive at launch; audiobook and direct deferred until the series is proven; then layer both for margin.**

---

## 1. Kindle Unlimited — ✅ PRIMARY CHANNEL, enroll exclusively

**Verdict: Enroll all FEED titles in KDP Select / KU from launch.**

| Factor | Assessment |
|---|---|
| **Reader fit** | Target reader is a **heavy KU subscriber**; psych/domestic thriller is **~73% KU-penetrated**. This is where the audience already reads. |
| **Read-through** | **KU borrowers read through series at higher rates than buyers** (next book is free to them). *FEED*'s short-chapter, cliffhanger, connected-standalone design is engineered to convert borrows into binges. |
| **Page-read revenue** | KENP ~**$0.0043/page**; an ~85k-word book ≈ **450 KENP ≈ ~$1.90 per full read** [est.], *stacking* across the series on top of any sales. |
| **Discovery** | A no-platform solo author gets **algorithmic discovery** from Amazon that wide retail can't replicate cold. |
| **Cost** | **Amazon exclusivity** — no Apple/Kobo/Google/wide for enrolled titles. *Accepted.* |

**Why the exclusivity trade is worth it here:** for a cold-start solo author, *discovery* is the binding constraint, and KU is the discovery engine for exactly this reader. Revisit wide only once a direct audience exists (below).

---

## 2. Audiobook — ⏸️ DEFER, then produce aggressively

**Verdict: No audio at launch. Produce Book 1 audio once Books 1–3 prove ebook/KU read-through.**

- **Why defer:** production is **$2,000–$5,000+ per title** (or a royalty-share that halves royalties *and* locks exclusivity). A poor first-year bet before lifetime value is proven. *(economics.md §5)*
- **Why *FEED* is a strong audio candidate later:** the book **is** a first-person obsessive voice — the intimate narrator is literally the product. Obsession thrillers with a magnetic POV over-perform in audio. A skilled narrator turns the "reader-proxy" voice into a binge format.
- **Model when triggered:** ACX (new pooled **50% exclusive / 30% non-exclusive**) *or* wide via **Voices by INaudio** (~20% cut) + **Spotify for Authors**. The interstitials (DMs, captions) need a light audio-adaptation pass (a second voice or sound cue) — plan for it.
- **Trigger:** strong read-through on Books 1–3 (proof of lifetime value) → produce audio starting with Book 1, then backfill.

---

## 3. Direct sales — ⏸️ DEFER to year 2+, then the margin play

**Verdict: Not a cold-start channel. Layer on once a newsletter audience exists.**

- **Why not at launch:** direct requires **you to drive your own traffic** — there's no Amazon browse/also-bought discovery, and it doesn't count toward Amazon rank. *(economics.md §6)*
- **Why it matters later:** selling direct keeps **~95%** vs 30–70% at retail, and you **own the customer** (email, purchase data, remarketing).
- **Sequence:** keep new releases on Amazon/KU for the launch algorithm boost; once the list exists, sell **box sets, special editions, and backlist direct** via **Payhip** (free → $29/mo Pro at ~$580/mo sales) + **BookFunnel** delivery.
- **Fit for *FEED*:** the series brand ("a new obsession every book") and a passionate BookTok fanbase are ideal for **direct box sets and limited special editions** — high-margin superfan products once the audience is warm.

---

## 4. Print (POD) — ✅ launch-adjacent, secondary

**Verdict: Publish paperback at launch for trust/anchoring; not a revenue driver.** $14.99 nets ~$4–5/copy; role is legitimacy, gift/signed copies, price-anchoring the ebook, and "also in paperback" credibility. Skip Expanded Distribution initially. *(economics.md §4)*

---

## 5. Channel roadmap

| Phase | KU | Ebook sales | Print | Audio | Direct |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Launch (Bk 1–3)** | ✅ exclusive | ✅ | ✅ | ❌ | ❌ |
| **Build (Bk 4–6 + box set)** | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ▶️ start Bk 1 | ▶️ box sets |
| **Scale (mature backlist)** | ✅ (or test wide) | ✅ | ✅ +ED | ✅ full series | ✅ storefront + special editions |

**Summary:** KU-first and exclusive to win discovery and read-through; print for trust; **defer audio and direct until lifetime value is proven**, then layer them as the margin-and-ownership plays. This sequencing matches indie thriller economics precisely and keeps first-year cash outlay low.
